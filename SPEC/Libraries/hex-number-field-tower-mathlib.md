# hex-number-field-tower-mathlib (depends on hex-number-field-tower + hex-number-field-mathlib + hex-resultant-mathlib + hex-berlekamp-zassenhaus-mathlib + hex-row-reduce-mathlib)

Mathlib companion for `hex-number-field-tower`. It interprets every validated
tower as a finite extension of `ℚ` with a fixed embedding into `ℂ`, proves the
coordinate field operations, and verifies Trager factorization, adjoining,
splitting fields, and primitive-element flattening.

## Semantic tower

Associate to each `T : NumberTower` a field `TowerField T`, an equivalence from
`NumberTower.Elem T`, and an embedding into `ℂ`.

```lean
def NumberTower.toField (T : NumberTower) : Type
def NumberTower.equivField (T : NumberTower) :
    NumberTower.Elem T ≃+* T.toField
def NumberTower.toComplex (T : NumberTower) :
    NumberTower.Elem T →+* ℂ

theorem NumberTower.toComplex_injective (T : NumberTower) :
    Function.Injective T.toComplex
```

The implementation may realize `toField` as iterated `AdjoinRoot` or as the
intermediate field generated by the stored complex generators. Public theorems
must hide that choice.

## Level invariant

For every validated level, prove:

1. the defining polynomial maps to an irreducible polynomial over the semantic
   lower field;
2. evaluating it at the stored absolute generator through the lower embedding
   gives zero;
3. the coordinate quotient is equivalent to adjoining that exact generator;
4. the resulting complex map is injective.

This is the central invariant. Irreducibility proves the quotient is a field;
the zero evaluation proves that its chosen embedding is the intended conjugate.
The private-constructor policy means induction over `rat`, `ofQAdjoin`, and
successful `adjoin?`/`split?` results suffices.

## Arithmetic correspondence

```lean
theorem NumberTower.map_add (T) (a b : T.Elem) :
    T.toComplex (a + b) = T.toComplex a + T.toComplex b
theorem NumberTower.map_mul (T) (a b : T.Elem) :
    T.toComplex (a * b) = T.toComplex a * T.toComplex b
theorem NumberTower.map_inv (T) (a : T.Elem) :
    T.toComplex a⁻¹ = (T.toComplex a)⁻¹
```

Also prove coordinate uniqueness, `dim T = finrank ℚ T.toField`, install the
`Lean.Grind.Field (NumberTower.Elem T)` law package for the computational
operations, and prove that every `Extension.embed` is an injective `ℚ`-algebra
homomorphism under its semantic interpretation.

## Trager factorization

```lean
theorem NumberTower.factor?_sound (T) (f : T.Poly) {r}
    (h : T.factor? f = some r) :
    Factorization.Sound T f r

theorem NumberTower.factor?_isSome (T) (f : T.Poly) :
    (T.factor? f).isSome
```

`Factorization.Sound` states reconstruction including the scalar, monicity,
positive multiplicities, irreducibility over `T.toField`, no associates, and
deterministic ordering.

The proof is organized by Yun squarefree component:

1. Transfer Yun decomposition and its multiplicity index through
   `equivField`.
2. At a level `K(α)/K`, identify the one-level executable resultant with the
   relative field norm by full resultant agreement and the root-product formula.
3. For `d = [K(α):K]` and `m = degree g`, prove that among the first
   `choose(d*m, 2) + 1` signed integer shifts, one relative norm is squarefree
   by counting collisions of its `d*m` conjugate roots.
4. Recursively factor that relative norm over `K`, reaching integer
   factorization only at the rational base tower.
5. Prove relative gcd recovery produces exactly the irreducible factors over
   `K(α)` and that the checked reconstruction predicate succeeds.
6. Reassemble the Yun components, preserving their multiplicities.

The induction is necessarily level-by-level. An absolute norm is not a valid
substitute: for example, in `ℚ(√2, √3)`, a factor defined over `ℚ(√3)` is
duplicated by the unused `√2` embeddings, so every absolute norm after shifting
only by `√3` has repeated factors.

The Stage 1 vanishing theorem from `hex-resultant-mathlib` is insufficient for
steps 2 and 5. These theorems depend on its Stage 2 full value correspondence.

## Adjoining

```lean
theorem NumberTower.adjoin?_sound (T) (a : AlgebraicRoot) {E}
    (h : T.adjoin? a = some E) :
    Extension.Sound T a E

theorem NumberTower.adjoin?_isSome (T) (a : AlgebraicRoot) :
    (T.adjoin? a).isSome
```

`Extension.Sound` states that `embed` preserves the old embedding, `gen` maps
to `a.toComplex`, the new tower is generated by the image and `gen`, and an
identity extension is returned exactly when `a.toComplex` already lies in the
embedded old tower.

Soundness follows from factor correctness and candidate evaluation.
Completeness of factor selection uses `evalDisambiguationPrec` and its
evaluation-eliminant lower bound for every nonvanishing factor.

## Splitting fields

```lean
theorem NumberTower.split?_sound (T) (f : T.Poly) {S}
    (h : T.split? f = some S) :
    Splitting.Sound T f S

theorem NumberTower.split?_isSome (T) (f : T.Poly) :
    (T.split? f).isSome
```

`Splitting.Sound` states that the inclusion preserves coefficients, the mapped
polynomial is reconstructed by the returned linear roots with the listed
multiplicities, and the extension is generated over `T` by those roots. The zero
polynomial is represented by `NumberTower.Roots.all` rather than a finite root
array.

Termination uses the sum of degrees of nonlinear factors after each genuine
adjoining step. Completeness uses algebraic closedness of `ℂ`, absolute
eliminant completeness, and `adjoin?_isSome`.

## Flattening

```lean
theorem NumberTower.flatten?_sound (T) {F}
    (h : T.flatten? = some F) :
    Function.LeftInverse F.fromPrimitive F.toPrimitive ∧
    Function.RightInverse F.fromPrimitive F.toPrimitive

theorem NumberTower.flatten?_isSome (T) :
    T.flatten?.isSome

theorem NumberTower.flatten_toComplex (T) {F}
    (h : T.flatten? = some F) (a : T.Elem) :
    QAdjoin.toComplex F.root.x (F.toPrimitive a) = T.toComplex a
```

The accepted full-degree eliminant proves the computed `γ` is primitive. Exact
row-reduction correspondence proves the recovered coordinate maps. Checking the
two maps on basis vectors extends to all elements by linearity, yielding the two
round trips. `AlgebraicRoot.exact_toComplex` identifies the canonical primitive
root stored in the result.

## Required developments

1. Iterated quotient-field semantics and fixed complex embeddings.
2. Coordinate basis, dimension, and arithmetic correspondence.
3. Resultant-as-norm for each tower level and recursively over the tower.
4. Characteristic-zero shift bounds and Trager gcd recovery.
5. Adjoin factor-selection soundness and embedding preservation.
6. Splitting termination, reconstruction, and generated-field minimality.
7. Primitive-element degree test and flattening coordinate equivalences.

Items 3 through 7 begin only after Stage 2 of `hex-resultant-mathlib` is
available.
