## Deliverables

1. In `HexGramSchmidt/Update.lean`, replace the `sorry` in
   `scaledCoeffs_adjacentSwap_pivot` (around line 855) with a real
   proof of:

   ```lean
   theorem scaledCoeffs_adjacentSwap_pivot (b : Matrix Int n m)
       (k : Fin n) (hk : 0 < k.val) :
       let km1 := GramSchmidt.prevRow k hk
       GramSchmidt.entry (scaledCoeffs (adjacentSwap b k hk)) k km1 =
         GramSchmidt.entry (scaledCoeffs b) k km1
   ```

   The integer LHS uses `gramDet (adjacentSwap b k hk) k.val` (the
   swap-updated pivot Gram determinant); the integer RHS uses
   `gramDet b k.val`. The rational cancellation of `dk' * coeffs` on
   the LHS against `gramDet b k * coeffs b` on the RHS should follow
   from `coeffs_adjacentSwap_pivot`'s rational identity together with
   the existing rational dot-product / Gram-det relationships in
   `HexGramSchmidt/Int.lean` (`scaledCoeffMatrix_det_eq_gramDet_mul_coeffs`
   and friends), then promoted to the integer cast via
   `intCast_rat_injective_local`.

   If a pure-rational version of the identity is the cleanest
   intermediate (and is genuinely needed for the bridge), introduce
   it as a private helper local to the same `namespace
   GramSchmidt.Int` section, but do not change the public statement.

2. Do not modify the existing seven sorries (the other four
   adjacent-swap sorries at lines 875, 881, 893, 899 and the two
   covered by #3886 at lines 773 and 779). Helper lemmas added to
   `HexGramSchmidt/Update.lean` to support deliverable 1 are fine.

3. Do not add `axiom`, `native_decide`, `TODO`, `FIXME`, or new
   theorem-level `sorry`.
