`HexGF2Mathlib` is in Phase 5 (`python3 scripts/status.py HexGF2Mathlib`

  lines ~132–152) — **out of scope here**.
- The packed binary **finite-index bijection** between `Hex.GF2Poly` and
  `Fin (2 ^ degree)` via `toNat` / `ofNatBelowDegree` (lines ~196–218) — **this

The index cluster is four theorems, all about the natural-number interpretation
`toNat : Hex.GF2Poly → Nat` (low-bit packing, `wordsToNatAux`) and its partial
inverse `ofNatBelowDegree : Nat → Nat → Hex.GF2Poly`:

```lean
theorem toNat_lt_of_degree_lt {p : Hex.GF2Poly} {degree : Nat}
    (h : p.IsZero ∨ p.degree < degree) : toNat p < 2 ^ degree := sorry
theorem ofNatBelowDegree_reduced (degree : Nat) (i : Fin (2 ^ degree)) :
    (ofNatBelowDegree degree i.1).IsZero ∨
      (ofNatBelowDegree degree i.1).degree < degree := sorry
theorem toNat_ofNatBelowDegree (degree : Nat) (i : Fin (2 ^ degree)) :
    toNat (ofNatBelowDegree degree i.1) = i.1 := sorry
theorem ofNatBelowDegree_toNat {p : Hex.GF2Poly} {degree : Nat}
    (h : p.IsZero ∨ p.degree < degree) :
    ofNatBelowDegree degree (toNat p) = p := sorry
```

This is the base-2 analogue of the FpPoly base-`p` index encode/decode
bijection just landed for `HexGFqMathlib` in
https://github.com/kim-em/hex/pull/7844 ("HexGFqMathlib Phase 5: prove the
FpPoly base-p index encode/decode bijection (Basic.lean)"); use that proof as a
shape reference. The cluster is independent of the `RingEquiv.symm` helper
(issue #7831) and of the `equiv` ring-correspondence lemmas — `toNat` packs
`UInt64` words directly and never touches `FpPoly`.

## Deliverables

1. Prove `toNat_lt_of_degree_lt` and `ofNatBelowDegree_reduced` (the two
   bound/reducedness directions): a degree-`< degree` (or zero) packed
   polynomial indexes below `2 ^ degree`, and decoding a bounded index yields a
   representative that is zero or has degree `< degree`.

2. Prove `toNat_ofNatBelowDegree` and `ofNatBelowDegree_toNat` (the two
   round-trips): encode-after-decode is the identity on `Fin (2 ^ degree)`, and
   decode-after-encode is the identity on reduced representatives. Together with
   deliverable 1 these establish the `GF2Poly ≃ Fin (2 ^ degree)` finite-index
   bijection for bounded-degree representatives.

3. Do **not** touch the `equiv` ring-correspondence sorries (`toFpPoly_one`,
   `toFpPoly_mul`, etc.) or any `Field.lean` sorry; those are separate Phase 5
   clusters. Leave the `wordsToNatAux` / `ofNatBelowDegree` definitions
   unchanged unless a proof genuinely requires a definitional adjustment, in
   which case keep it minimal and behaviour-preserving.

## Premise check (do before writing proofs)

Confirm the executable shapes line up before relying on the analogy to #7844:

- `ofNatBelowDegree degree n` uses `wordCount = (degree + 63) / 64` and
  `UInt64.ofNat (n / 2 ^ (64 * i))` per word — verify this truncates to the low
  `degree` bits as the round-trip claims (the high bits of the top word above
  `degree` must be forced zero by the `i < 2 ^ degree` bound, not by `degree`
  itself). If the top word can retain bits in `[degree, 64 * wordCount)` for a
  valid in-range index, `ofNatBelowDegree_reduced` may need the `< degree`
  refinement to come from the index bound rather than from `wordCount`; flag any
  such gap rather than weakening the statement.
- `toNat`/`wordsToNatAux` weight word `i` by `2 ^ (64 * i)`; make sure the
  `GF2Poly.degree` / `IsZero` predicates relate to these bits the way the bound
  proof assumes. If `degree` and the executable `GF2Poly.degree` disagree on the
  zero polynomial or on leading-word conventions, report the mismatch on the
  issue with the concrete unfolding instead of forcing the proof.

If the decomposition is sound, execute. If a statement is not provable as
written (e.g. missing hypothesis, off-by-one in the word/bit bound), stop and
comment on the issue with the counterexample or missing-lemma shape; do not add
a `sorry`/`axiom` or silently weaken a theorem.
