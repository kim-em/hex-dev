## Current state

`scripts/status.py` reports `HexBerlekamp -> Phase 6 (proof polishing)`.
`HexBerlekamp/Factor.lean` and `HexBerlekamp/Basic.lean` are sorry-free,
axiom-free, and `native_decide`-free today.

`HexBerlekamp/Factor.lean` owns the production Berlekamp-factor surface:
`SplitResult`, `Factorization`, `factorProduct`,
`Factorization.product`, `splitFactorAt`, `kernelWitnessSplit?`,
`berlekampFactor`, and the capstone product invariant
`prod_berlekampFactor`. `HexBerlekamp/Basic.lean` owns the foundational
helpers `basisSize`, `coeffVector`, `berlekampColumn`,
`berlekampMatrix`, `fixedSpaceMatrix`, `vectorToPoly`,
`fixedSpaceKernelVectors`, and `fixedSpaceKernel`.

`hex-berlekamp-zassenhaus` reuses `berlekampFactor` and its product
invariant as the F_p-side factor source; the recent HO-driven BZ work
benefits from a crisp upstream surface here.

"`hex-berlekamp` proves the computational invariant (no Mathlib): …
`prod_berlekampFactor (f : FpPoly p) (hf : squareFree f) :
(berlekampFactor f).prod = f`."

Q1: `HexBerlekamp/Factor.lean` is the canonical owner of the
production Berlekamp factor surface; `HexBerlekamp/Basic.lean` owns
the matrix/kernel helpers it consumes.
Q2: no Mathlib strategy is needed; this is a Mathlib-free
computational API review.
Q3: Mathlib has no theorem about Hex's `berlekampFactor` loop, its
`Factorization` record, or the project-local `splitFactorAt` shape.
Q4: no prerequisite issue is required. Keep this a review/polish
slice, not a new correctness campaign.
