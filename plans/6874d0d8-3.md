## Current state

`HexPolyMathlib/Basic.lean` provides the ring-equivalence bridge from
`Hex.DensePoly` to `Mathlib.Polynomial` with a wide simp surface
covering most constructors and operations on both sides of the
isomorphism:

- `@[simp]` `ofPolynomial_zero`, `ofPolynomial_one`, `ofPolynomial_C`,
  `ofPolynomial_neg`, `ofPolynomial_sub`, `ofPolynomial_add`,
  `ofPolynomial_monomial`, `ofPolynomial_mul`
- `@[simp]` `toPolynomial_zero`, `toPolynomial_one`, `toPolynomial_C`,
  `toPolynomial_monomial`, `toPolynomial_add`, `toPolynomial_neg`,
  `toPolynomial_sub`, `toPolynomial_mul`
- `@[simp]` `coeff_toPolynomial`, `coeff_ofPolynomial`,
  `natDegree_toPolynomial`, `leadingCoeff_toPolynomial`
- `equiv : Hex.DensePoly R ≃+* Polynomial R` and round-trip simp
  lemmas `toPolynomial_ofPolynomial`, `ofPolynomial_toPolynomial`,
  `equiv_apply`, `equiv_symm_apply`.

The bridge is missing the simp lemma for formal derivative — even
though `Hex.DensePoly.derivative` (in `HexPoly/Operations.lean`) and
`Mathlib.Polynomial.derivative` are both present and the bridge is
otherwise saturated for the elementary constructors and ring
operations. Downstream proofs that need to push `toPolynomial`
through a derivative must currently expand the bridge by hand.

## Deliverables

1. In `HexPolyMathlib/Basic.lean`, add a `@[simp]` bridge lemma for
   the formal derivative, placed adjacent to `toPolynomial_mul` and
   the other `toPolynomial_*` simp lemmas:

   ```lean
   @[simp]
   theorem toPolynomial_derivative [CommSemiring R] [DecidableEq R]
       (p : Hex.DensePoly R) :
       (p.derivative).toPolynomial = Polynomial.derivative p.toPolynomial := ...
   ```

   Typeclass shape (`Semiring R` vs. `CommSemiring R`) may be adjusted
   to whatever the existing `Polynomial.derivative` simp lemmas
   require for the analogous Mathlib statement; pick the weakest
   sufficient class and match the surrounding `toPolynomial_*` lemmas.

2. Discharge the proof via the existing
   `Polynomial.ext_iff`/`Polynomial.coeff_derivative` API on the
   Mathlib side and `Hex.DensePoly.coeff_derivative_semiring`
   (`HexPoly/Operations.lean`) on the Hex side. The proof should be
   short — a `Polynomial.ext` followed by `simp` with the existing
   `coeff_toPolynomial`, `coeff_derivative_semiring`, and
   `Polynomial.coeff_derivative` lemmas, possibly with a `Nat.cast`
   normalisation step.

3. Keep the change local to `HexPolyMathlib/Basic.lean`; do not modify
   `HexPolyMathlib/Euclid.lean`, `HexPoly/Operations.lean`, or
   benchmarks. Do not add the dual `ofPolynomial_derivative` lemma or
   any other new bridge simp lemmas in this issue — they are separate
   Phase 6 items.

## Library placement

Target file: `HexPolyMathlib/Basic.lean`.

SPEC section: `SPEC/Libraries/hex-poly-mathlib.md`:
"Proves the ring equivalence between `DensePoly R` and Mathlib's
`Polynomial R`: ... `def equiv [CommRing R] [DecidableEq R] :
DensePoly R ≃+* Polynomial R`".

Placement questions:

- Which SPEC section governs this file? `SPEC/Libraries/hex-poly-mathlib.md`,
  the ring-equivalence bridge between `Hex.DensePoly` and
  `Mathlib.Polynomial`.
- Does the natural strategy use Mathlib? Yes. The lemma talks about
  `Polynomial.derivative` on the Mathlib side, which is exactly why
  it belongs in the `*-mathlib` bridge library and not in `HexPoly`.
- Is this result already in Mathlib? No — Mathlib has
  `Polynomial.derivative_*` simp lemmas for its own type, but the
  bridge lemma `toPolynomial_derivative` is project-local because
  `Hex.DensePoly.derivative` is project-local. Strategy: implement
  locally, following the existing `toPolynomial_mul` pattern.
- Does the deliverable presuppose missing infrastructure? No.
  `Hex.DensePoly.derivative` and `Hex.DensePoly.coeff_derivative_semiring`
  exist in `HexPoly/Operations.lean`; `Polynomial.derivative` and
  `Polynomial.coeff_derivative` are in Mathlib;
  `coeff_toPolynomial` is already a simp lemma in
  `HexPolyMathlib/Basic.lean`.
