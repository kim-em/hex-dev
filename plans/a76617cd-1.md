## Deliverables

1. **`hfast_none` plumbing**. The branch-shape lemma takes
   `hfast_none : factorFastFactorsWithBound f B = none`, but its
   sibling `_small_mod_singleton_raw` takes `hB_gt_one` instead. The
   slow-path quadratic arm is reachable only when the fast path
   declines (e.g. `B = 0`, or `B = 1` with the fast-core check
   missing). Verify:

   - The choice of `hfast_none` over the inverted "slow path was
     selected" predicate is the cleanest premise — confirm there is
     no `factorWithBound_uses_slow_iff_factorFast_none` style
     theorem already in `HexBerlekampZassenhaus/Basic.lean` that
     would let the umbrella take a more compositional premise.
   - The eventual capstone assembly (HO-1 #4170) can discharge
     `hfast_none` cleanly when case-splitting on
     `factorFastFactorsWithBound f B`. Sketch the discharge path
     (case `none`: direct; case `some`: contradicts membership in
     `factorSlowFactorsWithBound`) and confirm no missing substrate.
   - Cross-check against #4571's branch-shape lemma (now in PR
     #4582) to see whether the fast-path quadratic arm takes a
     symmetric `hfast_some` premise. If asymmetric, document the
     reason; if symmetric, consider extracting a shared
     `factorWithBound_entry_quadratic_branch_raw` parametric in
     fast/slow dispatch.

2. **Classifier introduction — promote or specialise**. The PR
   introduces
   `reassemblePolynomialFactors_quadratic_irreducible_or_residual_of_expansionComplete`

   worked, but the worker chose to fold the case split into a
   cleaner two-way form). Verify:

   - The new classifier is the correct level of granularity for
     reuse. The fast-path quadratic arm (#4571, PR #4582) consumes
     the same `hquad` shape and the same `hcomplete` predicate, so
     the two arm umbrellas should share this classifier verbatim.
     Confirm PR #4582 in fact uses
     `_quadratic_irreducible_or_residual_of_expansionComplete`
     rather than re-deriving the disjunction inline.
   - The naming
     `_quadratic_irreducible_or_residual_of_expansionComplete`
     follows the sibling
     `_mem_xPower_or_core_of_expansionComplete` style. Confirm.
   - The pre-existing
     `_quadratic_irreducible_or_repeated_or_residual` classifier
     (immediately below, line 7674) is still useful for callers
     that do not have `hcomplete`. Verify it has at least one
     remaining caller; if not, consider whether it should be
     derived from the new classifier (or vice-versa) to avoid
     proof duplication.

   - Keep the suffix uniformly across arms (parity argument) — and
     document the convention in a comment near one umbrella so
     future readers do not chase the spurious dependency.

     consume `choosePrimeData` (constant, slow-quadratic).

   review (#4577) concludes; coordinate or defer to that resolution.
