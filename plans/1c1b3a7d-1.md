## Current state

`scripts/status.py` reports `HexGfqRing -> Phase 6 (proof polishing)`.
`HexGfqRing/Basic.lean` and `HexGfqRing/Operations.lean` are
sorry-free, axiom-free, and `native_decide`-free today.

`HexGfqRing/Basic.lean` owns the foundational quotient-ring surface:
`degree`, `reduceMod`, `IsReduced`, the canonical `PolyQuotient f hf`
type abbreviation, the smart constructor `ofPoly`, the projection
`repr`, the `DecidableEq` instance, and the `reduceMod` /
`ofPoly` congruence and idempotence lemmas (`reduceMod_idem`,
`reduceMod_self`, `reduceMod_*_reduceMod`, `ofPoly_reduceMod`,
`degree_repr_lt_degree`, `eq_zero_iff_repr_eq_zero`,
`ne_zero_iff_repr_ne_zero`).

`HexGfqRing/Operations.lean` owns the ring operations and
`Lean.Grind.CommRing` instance: `zero`, `one`, `const`, `add`, `mul`,
`neg`, `sub`, `pow`, `natCast`, `nsmul`, `intCast`, `zsmul`, the
matching `Zero/One/Add/Mul/Neg/Sub/Pow` typeclass instances, and the
ring-axiom lemmas required by `Grind.CommRing`.

Per `SPEC/Libraries/hex-gfq-ring.md`, the public quotient type
carries a hypothesis `0 < FpPoly.degree f` so the
canonical-representative invariant "degree < deg(f)" is meaningful
without special-casing constant moduli, and every operation reduces
through `reduceMod` with the `pow`/`nsmul`/`natCast` complexity
constraints from the spec.
