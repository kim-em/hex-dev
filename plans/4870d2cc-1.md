## Context

### Why this matters

After #4551 closes (in flight as PR #4552), every substrate constructor
for the slow-path arm of the HO-1 capstone (`factor_irreducible_of_nonUnit`,
#4170) is dischargeable from `choosePrimeData` / `henselLiftData`
invariants — *except* for `hcore_sqfree`, which is the last remaining
"please supply this from your own invariants" hypothesis on the
outer-bound surface. Closing this gap finishes the substrate layer for
the slow-path arm of #4170 and unblocks a partial HO-1 assembly
(leaving only the analytic `sorry`s tracked by directive #2567 / BHKS
Theorem 5.2).

### Proof strategy sketch

The classical mathematical fact: a primitive polynomial `g : Polynomial ℤ`
is squarefree iff its image `g.map (algebraMap ℤ ℚ) : Polynomial ℚ`
is squarefree (Gauss's-lemma-style transfer). The proof has two halves:

* **Bridge `SquareFreeRat g → Squarefree (toRatPoly g)`** in the
  `HexPolyZ` / `HexPolyZMathlib` surface. The executable side already
  records that the rational gcd of `toRatPoly g` and its derivative is
  a unit; the Mathlib characterisation of squarefreeness over a field
  (e.g., `Polynomial.squarefree_iff_isCoprime_derivative` or
  `EuclideanDomain.gcd_isUnit_iff` style) closes this half. A
  `toRatPoly`-side Mathlib bridge analogous to the existing
  `coeff_toPolynomial` may be needed if no such bridge already exists.

* **Drop from `Polynomial ℚ` to `Polynomial ℤ` for primitives.** Use
  `Polynomial.IsPrimitive`-based transfer (cf. how Mathlib's
  `Polynomial.IsPrimitive.Int.irreducible_iff` routes primitivity facts
  between ℤ and ℚ). The primitivity of `(normalizeForFactor f).squareFreeCore`
  is already available via
  `normalizeForFactor_squareFreeCore_toPolynomial_isPrimitive`.

The worker may choose any other route that closes the deliverable
honestly (no new `sorry`s, no `axiom`).
