Surface 2 owns the bridge between `FpPoly p` (computational base ring
modulo `p`) and `ZPoly` (centred integer lifts) used by the linear
Hensel step's correctness proof. Public theorems in this surface:

- `modP_one` (line 169): `ZPoly.modP p (1 : ZPoly) = (1 : FpPoly p)`.
- `congr_liftToZ_of_modP_eq` (line 200): converts a `modP` equality
  into a `ZPoly.congr` against `liftToZ`.
- `liftToZ_add_congr` (line 242):
  `liftToZ (f + g) ≡ liftToZ f + liftToZ g (mod p)`.
- `liftToZ_mul_congr` (line 486):
  `liftToZ (f * g) ≡ liftToZ f * liftToZ g (mod p)`.
- `modP_add` (line 499): `modP p (z + w) = modP p z + modP p w`.
- `modP_lift_mul_left` (line 525):
  `modP p (liftToZ r * h) = r * modP p h`.
- `modP_lift_mul_right` (line 545):
  `modP p (g * liftToZ hCorrection) = modP p g * hCorrection`.
- `modP_add_lift_mul` (line 569): combines `modP_add`,
  `modP_lift_mul_left`, `modP_lift_mul_right` into the form consumed
  by the linear Hensel step proof.

The private body of the surface is a `mulCoeff`-diagonal expansion
chain that proves `liftToZ_mul_congr`:

- `zmod_add_lift_congr`, `zmod_add_zero_zero`, `zmod_mul_lift_congr`
  (toNat ↔ ofNat congruences for `ZMod64 p`).
- `liftToZ_mulCoeffTerm_congr` (per-term coefficient lift).
- `intDiagonalMulCoeffTerm` / `intBoundedDiagonalMulCoeffTerm`
  (private definitions reifying the diagonal sum into `Int`).
- `fold_mulCoeffStep_eq_bounded_diagonal_int`,
  `fold_mulCoeffStep_eq_diagonal_int`,
  `fold_mulCoeff_outer_eq_diagonal_int`,
  `mulCoeffSum_eq_diagonal_int`,
  `intDiagonalMulCoeffTerm_eq_zero_of_size_le`,
  `fold_diagonal_extend_int`, `diagonalSum_eq_bound_int`
  (fold-shape lemmas reducing `DensePoly.mulCoeffSum` to a diagonal
  sum of `intDiagonalMulCoeffTerm`).
- `liftToZ_size_le`, `liftToZ_mulCoeffTerm_diagonal_congr`,
  `zmod_add_lift_congr_of_terms`, `fold_liftToZ_mulCoeffTerm_congr`
  (the lift transport carrying the per-term congruence over the
  diagonal sum).

The public theorems are short; the polish work is concentrated on
docstring coverage for the eight public statements, an audit of which
private helpers qualify as "non-obvious" under
`SPEC/design-principles.md`, and a `@[simp]` audit limited to
unconditional canonical-form public rewrites.
