## Current state

`HexGramSchmidt/Basic.lean` lines ~724–829 contain six undocumented
private lemmas characterising how `subtractProjection` and
`reduceAgainstBasis` behave on zero inputs, on rows already orthogonal
to the basis, and on a basis row reduced against itself. They are part
of the Phase 6 docstring-coverage pass (`PLAN/Phase6.md`: "docstring
coverage meets the rule in `SPEC/design-principles.md`"). The
declarations:

- `subtractProjection_zero_left` — `subtractProjection 0 b = 0`.
- `reduceAgainstBasis_zero_left` — `reduceAgainstBasis basisRev 0 = 0`.
- `subtractProjection_eq_self_of_dot_zero` — projecting out a basis row
  the input is already orthogonal to leaves the input unchanged.
- `reduceAgainstBasis_eq_self_of_forall_dot_zero` — list version: a row
  orthogonal to every basis vector survives the whole reduction.
- `subtractProjection_self_eq_zero` — `subtractProjection b b = 0`.
- `reduceAgainstBasis_cons_self_eq_zero` — reducing a basis row against
  a prefix that begins with it yields `0`.
