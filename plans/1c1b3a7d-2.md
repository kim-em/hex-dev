## Current state

`scripts/status.py` reports `HexBerlekamp -> Phase 6 (proof polishing)`.
`HexBerlekamp/Irreducibility.lean` is sorry-free, axiom-free, and
`native_decide`-free today.

`HexBerlekamp/Irreducibility.lean` owns the production Rabin
irreducibility-test and certificate-checker surface:

- foundational helpers `frobeniusDiffMod`, `properDivisors`,
  `maximalProperDivisors`, `isUnitPolynomial`;
- the rank-based primality predicate `berlekampRankTest` and its
  spec `berlekampRankTest_spec`;
- the Rabin tests `rabinDividesTest`, `rabinCoprimeTest`,
  `rabinWitnesses`, `rabinTest` and the spec
  `rabinDividesTest_spec`;
- the certificate types `IrreducibilityCertificate`,
  `SamePrimeIrreducibilityCertificate`, `RabinBezoutWitness`,
  and the projector `IrreducibilityCertificate.toAmbient?`;
- the checker surface `checkPowChain`, `checkPowChainLinear`,
  `checkPowChainLinearIncrementalStep`,
  `checkPowChainLinearIncremental`,
  `checkRabinBezoutWitness`, `checkRabinBezoutWitnesses`,
  `checkIrreducibilityCertificate`,
  `checkIrreducibilityCertificateLinear`, with their specs
  `checkPowChain_spec`, `checkPowChainLinear_spec`,
  `checkIrreducibilityCertificate_rabinTest`,
  `checkIrreducibilityCertificateLinear_rabinTest`.

`hex-berlekamp-mathlib` and `hex-gfq-field` consume the
certificate API and the `rabinTest` predicate; the recent HO-driven
HexConway work also relies on a crisp upstream Rabin surface here.
