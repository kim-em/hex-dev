The starting state is `(c, w) = (f/g, g)` where `g = gcd f f'`. The
classical Yun invariant is that at iteration i, `c` is the product of
distinct irreducible factors of `f` that appear with multiplicity at
least `i`, hence `c` is square-free, hence `z = c / gcd c w` is
square-free. The proof needs only the
`DensePoly.gcd z (derivative z) = 1` framing of square-freeness, not the
full multiplicity statement.
