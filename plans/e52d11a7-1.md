## Current state

The Phase 6 "grind automation coverage" sweep has landed across most of the
codebase — roughly 25 of the last 40 commits on `main` are grind-coverage
PRs (e.g. #8100/#8102/#8105/#8107/#8109/#8110/#8111 and the in-flight
#8113). The sweep promotes equation-shaped public `@[simp]` lemmas (literal
`lhs = rhs`, possibly under hypotheses) to `@[simp, grind =]`, deliberately
leaving `↔`, `<`, `≤`, `Ne`, `Associated`, conjunction, `let`-binder, and
`private` forms as `@[simp]`-only.

No single pass has audited the *result* of this multi-PR sweep as a whole.
Each PR self-verified its own file, but cross-PR consistency (uniform
exclusion criteria, no accidentally-promoted ineligible lemma, no
file that was skipped while a sibling was swept) has not been checked.

This is a read-and-verify audit. It is annotation-only at most (revert a
bad promotion / add a missing one), and it is disjoint from the HO-1
directive (#2564, the van Hoeij CLD recombination rewrite) and its blocked
chain (#4818/#6779/#8068), which live in `HexBerlekampZassenhaus` /
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib` proof bodies, not in `@[simp]`/`grind`
attributes.

## Deliverables

1. **No ineligible promotion.** Confirm every lemma carrying `grind =` has
   a literal `lhs = rhs` conclusion. In particular, scan for `grind =`
   lemmas whose *conclusion* (not a hypothesis) is `↔`, `<`, `≤`, `Ne`, a
   conjunction, `Associated`, or a `let`-binder. (Note: a `0 < degree f`
   *hypothesis* is fine — only the conclusion shape disqualifies.) If any
   ineligible lemma was promoted, revert it to `@[simp]`-only and record
   which and why in the progress file.

2. **No missed eligible lemma.** Spot-check the files that still carry
   plain `@[simp]`-only public lemmas (e.g. `HexModArith/Ring.lean`'s
   ring-axiom lemmas, `HexArith/ExtGcd.lean`'s `_spec`/`_bezout` lemmas)
   and confirm each is *intentionally* excluded (redundant with a derived
   `CommRing` grind rule, conjunction, iff, etc.), not an oversight. Where
   a genuinely eligible equation lemma was missed, either promote it (if
   trivially safe and terminating) or note it for a follow-up issue.

3. **No grind-loop / elaboration regression.** `lake build` the swept
   libraries clean and confirm no module shows a grind-loop slowdown.
   Report any module whose elaboration time looks anomalous.

Do not change proof bodies, signatures, or imports beyond reverting/adding
an attribute. No `sorry`/`axiom`/`native_decide`. Do not touch any file in
the HO-1 directive blast radius (`HexBerlekampZassenhaus*`).

## Context

The risk this audit guards against: `grind =` on a non-equation lemma is
either rejected by Lean (build break — would already be caught) or, worse,
accepted on a form that E-matches too broadly and introduces a grind loop
that only bites a downstream goal. A whole-sweep audit is the cleanup-phase
counterpart to the per-file feature PRs. Established sweep precedent and
exclusion criteria are documented in the closed issues #8014 (Ring.lean,
which explains why the ring-axiom lemmas stay `@[simp]`-only) and #7974
(ExtGcd.lean).

Useful starting scan:

```
grep -rn 'grind =' --include='*.lean' Hex* | grep -E '↔|<->'
```

then read each hit's full statement to classify conclusion vs. hypothesis.
