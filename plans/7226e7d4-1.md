## Per-site breakdown

All three sites share the same idiom: an `obtain ⟨_, hX, hY⟩ :=
monic_primitive_sign_normalized_of_monic h` destructures the triple,
then consumes both fields **positionally** (not through a `simpa`
adapter), either in an `exact ⟨...⟩` existential tuple literal or as
named arguments to a theorem call. The rewire replaces the single
`obtain` with two explicitly-typed `have`s.

The type ascription on `hX` is `Hex.ZPoly.content f = 1` rather than
`Hex.ZPoly.Primitive f` because the consumer expects the unfolded
form (see `recombinationCandidate_eq_factor_of_recovery_of_monic_core`
at `Basic.lean:4208`, parameter `hfactor_prim : Hex.ZPoly.content
factor = 1`; the existential tuple literals at 10086 and 10196
likewise occupy slots typed as `content _ = 1`). `Primitive` is a
non-`@[reducible]` `def`, so the ascription's def-eq unfold is the
deliberate documentation of which equality is being passed.
