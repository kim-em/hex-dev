### Design decision

The new wrapper takes the Mignotte invariant in its native
core-shape rather than a redundant outer-shape
`2 * defaultFactorCoeffBound f < d.p^d.k`.  Reasons:

1. The core-shape is the form the upstream theorem actually consumes,
   so the wrapper threads the hypothesis verbatim.
2. Constructing the core-shaped invariant from the outer-shaped one
   requires the executable-layer monotonicity
   `defaultFactorCoeffBound core ≤ defaultFactorCoeffBound f` (the
   issue's deliverable 1).  Keeping the wrapper signature aligned
   with the sibling lets the HO-1 capstone consumer (#4170) decide
   between (a) supplying the core invariant directly from a custom
   precision constructor, or (b) discharging it via the monotonicity
   once landed.

## Next step

Deliverable 1 (the monotonicity lemma
`defaultFactorCoeffBound (normalizeForFactor f).squareFreeCore ≤
defaultFactorCoeffBound f`) was attempted and deferred to a separate
issue.

The standard chain
`coeffL2NormBound g ≤ coeffL2NormBound f` combined with
`deg g ≤ deg f` would discharge a generic monotonicity for
`defaultFactorCoeffBound`, but the L2-norm half *fails* for
`squareFreeCore` in general: Mignotte allows a factor's L2 coefficient
norm to exceed the parent polynomial's by a factor of up to
`ceilSqrt(deg sqp + 1) · binom(deg sqp, ⌊deg sqp / 2⌋)`.  Empirically
the monotonicity always holds (every concrete case I checked produced
a smaller squareFreeCore bound), but a Mathlib-free proof would need
either Mahler-measure machinery or a custom analytic argument
specific to the squareFreeCore construction.  Since the wrapper takes
the Mignotte invariant as input rather than deriving it from the
outer `B`, the wrapper itself is complete without the monotonicity;
the consumer (#4170) can either prove the monotonicity later or
construct the core-shaped invariant by a different route.
