## Next step

The remaining obstacles for the full recursive theorem are:

1. **Result content vs. isSome.** The existing matched-rest bridges
   (including PR #4346's `_candidate_eq_factor_of_matches` and this
   PR's `_factor_dvd_of_matches`) prove `isSome`, not the `Associated`
   emitted conclusion. PR #4349 adds a first-success-witness
   variant that does give the `Associated` conclusion, but it requires
   a `hprefix` hypothesis: every split enumerated before
   `S_{f_min}` returns `none`.

2. **Prefix-none under partition.** Discharging `hprefix` requires
   showing that for any `selected'` enumerated before `S_{f_min}` in
   `subsetSplitsWithFirst`, either `shouldRecord(candidate')` fails
   or `exactQuotient? target candidate' = none`. Under Mignotte
   precision, this is equivalent to: `selected'` is not a union of
   representing subsets of irreducible divisors of `target`. The
   partition's `pairwise_disjoint` + `unique_up_to_associated`
   establish that `S_{f_min}` is the unique minimum-cardinality such
   union containing `J.min'`; combined with the subset-order
   preservation of `subsetSplits`, every "earlier" selected fails to
   contain all of `S_{f_min}` and so is not a valid union. The
   centered-lift-doesn't-divide-target argument for non-valid unions
   then closes the prefix-none obligation.

3. **Normalised cover witness.** `cover_at_min` provides an
   irreducible `f_min` with `f_min ∣ target` and
   `RepresentsIntegerFactorAtLift core d f_min S`, but doesn't supply
   `Hex.ZPoly.content f_min = 1` and
   `Hex.normalizeFactorSign f_min = f_min`, which the recovery lemma
   needs. For divisors of a monic primitive `target`, the normalised
   representative is unique up to sign; deriving it for `f_min` is a
   small but necessary step.
