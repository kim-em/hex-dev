## Accomplished

Claimed and implemented #2410 in `HexGF2/Field.lean`: the packed
quotient `GF2nPoly` now exposes the multiplication-permutation theorem
`nonzeroElements_map_mul_left_perm` plus the supporting commutative-ring

New theorems (in `HexGF2/Field.lean`, namespace `Hex.GF2nPoly`):

- `mul_val`, `one_val`, `zero_val`: bridges relating quotient values to
  reduced polynomial representatives (`(a * b).val = (a.val * b.val) % f`,
  `(1 : GF2nPoly).val = 1 % f`, `(0 : GF2nPoly).val = 0`).
- `mul_zero`, `zero_mul`: zero is absorbing.
- `mul_comm`, `mul_assoc`: multiplication is commutative and associative.
- `one_mul`, `mul_one`: `1` is a two-sided identity.
- `inv_mul_cancel`: left version of the existing `mul_inv_cancel` (via
  `mul_comm`).
- `mul_ne_zero_of_ne_zero`: nonzero × nonzero is nonzero (uses inverse +
  `one_mul` + `mul_assoc` + `mul_zero`).
- `mul_left_injective`: left multiplication by nonzero is injective.
- `nonzeroElements_map_mul_left_perm`: mapping `nonzeroElements` by
  left multiplication by a nonzero element is a permutation of the
  original list.

Two new private helpers: `GF2Poly_mulXk_zero` and `GF2Poly_one_mul`
(the polynomial-level identity `(1 : GF2Poly) * p = p`, since the
existing `Hex.GF2Poly.one_mul`/`mul_one` are file-private to
`HexGF2/Euclid.lean`); plus `val_mod_eq` (reducedness of `.val`) and
`perm_of_nodup_mem_iff` (two `Nodup` lists with the same membership
predicate are permutations, copied from `HexPolyFp/Quotient.lean`).

The proof shape mirrors the existing `HexPolyFp/Quotient.lean`
permutation theorem: nodup of the mapped list (via injectivity), plus
membership equivalence (via `mul_ne_zero_of_ne_zero` and the inverse
`a⁻¹ * c` round-trip).
