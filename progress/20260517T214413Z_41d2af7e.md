## Empirical findings (under contention)

Combined sweep ran for ~38 minutes wallclock on `carica` while another
worker was running the parametric scientific benchmarks for #4334 and
load averages were 8.97/10.67/11.53 at sweep start (rising to
~14 during the harsh-cubic tail). Absolute Lean and Isabelle wall times
in the report are inflated by an estimated 2-3× relative to a quiet
host; the Lean/Isabelle ratio on each rung is much less affected
because both sides share the contention.

Headline numbers (adjusted ratio, Lean/Isabelle):

| n   | random-bounded | harsh-cubic |
|----:|---------------:|------------:|
| 15  | n/a            | 0.0136      |
| 30  | 0.2014         | 0.2202      |
| 45  | 0.4396         | 1.0806      |
| 60  | 0.6840         | -           |
| 90  | 0.7982         | -           |
| 120 | 0.8449         | -           |
| 180 | 0.8403         | -           |
| 55  | -              | 1.4914      |

Both gating families show a climbing trend; harsh-cubic crosses the
parity line and reaches Lean-is-1.5×-slower at the largest eligible
rung. Even granting 2-3× contention inflation skews the absolute
times, the trend across the eligible range is itself an audit-found
Concern per the post-#3657 SPEC. `HexLLL.done_through` stays at `3`.
