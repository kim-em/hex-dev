# Progress: #6977 — bridge cldQuotientMod to logarithmic-derivative congruence

## Accomplished

Claimed and executed #6977 (brick 2 of the corrected CLD resultant-valuation
decomposition, replacement chain for #6971; depends on #6976, merged as #6995).

Added `HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib.cldQuotientMod_congr_mul_derivative` to
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib/CLDColumnBound.lean`. The theorem proves the
executable CLD quotient congruence:

```
input ≡ g * h (mod p^k), g monic of positive degree
  ⟹  g * cldQuotientMod input g p k ≡ input * g'  (mod p^k)
```

stated in the `Hex.ZPoly.congr` language and proven by transporting the monic
division to `Polynomial (ZMod (p^k))`:

- `Hex.ZPoly.divMod_reconstruction_of_monic` gives `q*g + r = numerator` over
  `ZPoly` (the #6976 reconstruction);
- mapping through `HexHenselMathlib.zpoly_congr_*toPolynomial_map_eq` and
  `congr_reduceModPow` reduces `reduceModPow` to identity modulo `p^k`;
- `Polynomial.div_modByMonic_unique` plus the executable remainder degree bound
  (`divMod_remainder_degree_lt_of_pos_degree_core`, transported via
  `natDegree_toPolynomial` / `degree_map_le`) identifies the mapped remainder
  with `numerator %ₘ (toPolynomial g).map φ`;
- divisibility of `input` by `g` makes the mapped numerator divisible by the
  mapped `g`, so `modByMonic_eq_zero_iff_dvd` forces the remainder to vanish.

The hypothesis is divisibility of `input` itself, **not** of the auxiliary
polynomial (per the issue's explicit warning). The docstring records the
composition with `BHKS.phi_eq_factor_mul_derivative`.
