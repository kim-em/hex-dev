### Audit findings

- **Style / docstrings.** All public declarations in
  `HexGfqRing/Basic.lean` and `HexGfqRing/Operations.lean` carry
  docstrings; private helpers are either trivial unfolding lemmas or
  documented (the `linearNSmul` / `linearPow` binary-decomposition
  bridges have explanatory docstrings from the #4496 / #4495 reviews).
  All declarations live under the `Hex.GFqRing` namespace per
  `SPEC/design-principles.md`. No `sorry` / `axiom` / `native_decide` /
  `TODO` / `FIXME` matches under `HexGfqRing` or `HexGfqRing.lean`.
- **Performance baseline.** `reports/hex-gfq-ring-performance.md`
  records a scientific run at `58fc57b3` on `carica` covering all
  seven Phase-4 bench targets, all verdicts consistent with declared
  complexity, Concerns section empty. The intervening HexGfqRing
  changes (#4476, #4481, #4483, #4484, #4492, #4493, #4495, #4506)
  are pure docstring / comment polish (`git diff
  58fc57b3..HEAD -- HexGfqRing/` shows no semantic operational
  changes), so the baseline remains the current comparator.
- **Native-decide-free / no irreducibility claims.** No
  `native_decide` use in the library. Per
  `SPEC/Libraries/hex-gfq-ring.md` §"External comparators", HexGfqRing
  is structural-layer: it exposes the quotient ring `F_p[x]/(f)` for
  *any* nonconstant modulus, including reducible ones, and makes no
  irreducibility or field-construction claim. The Lean-checked
  irreducibility criterion is therefore vacuous here.
- **Dead declarations.** The grep-driven sweep across `HexGfqRing/`,
  `HexGfqField/`, `HexGfq/`, `HexConway/`, the bench / fixture
  targets, and `HexGfqMathlib/` flagged seven public theorems with no
  in-tree callers:
  - `reduceMod_add_reduceMod` (an explicit alias of
    `reduceMod_add_reduceMod_congr`)
  - `ofPoly_add_reduceMod`
  - `ofPoly_mul_reduceMod`
  - `ofPoly_neg_reduceMod`
  - `ofPoly_sub_reduceMod`
  - `repr_add_neg_self`
  - `repr_sub_self`
  All seven were removed (-68 lines in `HexGfqRing/Operations.lean`).
  The mirror lemmas that *are* called downstream are kept:
  `reduceMod_mul_reduceMod` (used in `HexGfqField/Operations.lean`),
  `reduceMod_add_mul_self_right` (same), `repr_neg_add_self` (used in
  the `Lean.Grind.Ring` instance), and the `eq_zero_iff` / `ne_zero_iff`
  pair (the `ne_zero` direction is used by `HexGfqField`).
