# BHKS subset-descent proof strategy audit

## Accomplished

Audited the proposed proof strategy for the BHKS Hensel subset-descent lemma
around `liftedRecoveryCandidate`, `RepresentsIntegerFactorAtLift`,
`henselLiftData_liftedSubset_complement_isCoprime_mod_p`, and
`liftedFactorProduct_eq_mul_sdiff_of_subset`.

Identified the main delicate obligation: proving that reduction modulo `p` of
`primitivePart (dilate lc (centeredLiftPoly product))` is a unit multiple of the
variable-dilated mod-`p` product. This is sound when the selected lifted product
has nonzero monic mod-`p` reduction and `p ∤ lc`, because then the dilated
centered lift has at least one coefficient not divisible by `p`, so its content
is a unit modulo `p`.

## Current frontier

No Lean files were changed. The recommended decomposition is a candidate-to-mod
product transfer lemma plus a separate mod-`p` squarefree product support lemma.
The transfer lemma is the genuinely hard half; the support lemma should be
mostly finite-product/coprimality bookkeeping.

## Next step

If implementing, first prove small transport lemmas for centered lift modulo
`p`, primitive-part reduction modulo a prime when content is prime-coprime, and
variable-dilation as a `ZMod p` polynomial automorphism. Then use those to state
the transfer lemma cleanly before attacking the finite-product support lemma.

## Blockers

None hard for the strategy, but the proof needs explicit hypotheses/data for
the good-prime `p ∤ leadingCoeff core`, nonzero monic mod-`p` factor products,
and the existing Hensel/mod-`p` factor alignment. Without those, the primitive
part transfer step is under-specified.
