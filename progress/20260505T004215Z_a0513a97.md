# HexGF2 single-word product bridge

## Accomplished

- Claimed #2122 and confirmed the first two targeted inverse bridge theorems
  are already landed on `origin/main`.
- Added public normalization simp lemmas for zero and nonzero single-word
  `ofWords` arrays in `HexGF2/Basic.lean`.
- Added `GF2Poly.ofUInt64_mul_ofUInt64` in `HexGF2/Multiply.lean`, bridging
  multiplication of two single-word polynomials to the two-word `clmul`
  product representation.
- Verified `lake build HexGF2.Multiply`, `lake build HexGF2.Euclid`,
  `lake build HexGF2`, `python3 scripts/status.py HexGF2`,
  `python3 scripts/check_dag.py`, `git diff --check`, and scans of the touched
  Lean files for forbidden placeholders.

## Current frontier

#2122 remains partially complete. The final theorem
`packedReduceWord_clmul_packedInvWord_eq_one` still needs the bridge from a
reduced polynomial remainder to its low-word `packedReduceWord` representation.

## Next step

Prove a representation lemma for reduced single-word residues, likely stating
that when `p % ofUInt64Monic irr n` is below degree `n < 64`, its low stored
word reconstructs the reduced polynomial. Then combine it with
`ofUInt64_mul_ofUInt64` and
`mul_mod_xgcd_left_mod_eq_one_of_irreducible_of_nonzero_reduced`.

## Blockers

- The current statement of `packedReduceWord_clmul_packedInvWord_eq_one`
  only assumes `w ≠ 0`, while the issue text says nonzero canonical
  representatives. The eventual proof likely needs an explicit canonical
  bound such as `w.toNat < 2 ^ n` and `0 < n`, which `GF2n.mul_inv_cancel`
  already has available through the field element.
- `lake build HexGF2` still reports the pre-existing `sorry` in
  `HexGF2/Euclid.lean` and the separate pre-existing `sorry` in
  `HexGF2/Conformance.lean`.
