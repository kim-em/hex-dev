## Accomplished

Proved the monic-divisor Euclidean reconstruction identity that CLD
quotient work needs, and exposed it to `cldQuotientMod` callers.

- `Hex.ZPoly.divMod_reconstruction_of_monic` (`HexPolyZ/Basic.lean`):
  for monic `candidate` over `ZPoly = DensePoly Int`,
  `quot * candidate + rem = target` for *any* dividend. This is the
  monic specialization over `Int` of `DensePoly.divMod_reconstruction`
  — whose own docstring (`HexPoly/Euclid.lean:3753`) notes its
  leading-coefficient cancellation invariant
  `∀ a, a - (a / lc) * lc = 0` "only holds in the monic case over
  `Int`". The cancellation discharges by `rw [hmonic]; omega` (lc = 1,
  `a - (a/1)*1 = 0`). Unlike the existing
  `divMod_eq_of_monic_mul_eq` / `divMod_remainder_eq_zero_of_monic_mul_eq`,
  no exact-multiple hypothesis is needed, so the remainder may be
  nonzero — exactly the case for the CLD numerator, which is generally
  not divisible by `g`.
- `Hex.cldQuotientMod_divMod_reconstruction`
  (`HexBerlekampZassenhaus/Basic.lean`, next to `cldQuotientMod`): thin
  wrapper specializing the above to the dividend `cldQuotientMod` feeds
  to `DensePoly.divMod`, namely `numerator = (f * g') mod p^a`. Exposes
  `quot * g + rem = numerator` for monic `g` — the orientation the next
  modular-congruence bridge reduces mod `p^a`.
- Refactored `divMod_remainder_eq_zero_of_monic_mul_eq` and
  `divMod_eq_of_monic_mul_eq` to route their reconstruction step through
  the new lemma, removing duplicated `hcancel` + `divMod_reconstruction`
  boilerplate (the former keeps `hcancel` for its degree bound).
