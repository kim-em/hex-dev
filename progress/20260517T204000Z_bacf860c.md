the #4759 `_isFactorPower_polyProduct_` cluster.

The lemma states: given an irreducible cover of
`(Hex.normalizeForFactor f).squareFreeCore`, splitting
`coreFactors.toList.zip exponents` at any position `pre ++ (q, e) :: suf`
yields `¬ q ∣ ∏ factorPower q_j e_j` over the suffix. This is exactly
the precondition consumed by the exhaustive arm of
`Hex.expandRepeatedPartFactorArray_residual_eq_one_of_factorPower_decomposition`,
and is the list-shaped generalisation of the singleton-suffix
`Hex.irreducible_not_dvd_one` (`¬ q ∣ 1`).

Proof strategy (Mathlib bridge, no executable polynomial arithmetic):

1. Transport the irreducible cover product to `Polynomial ℤ` via
   `polyProduct_toPolynomial` and `normalizeForFactor_squareFreeCore_toPolynomial_squarefree`.
2. Combine the existing `List.nodup_of_prod_squarefree` helper with the
   transported irreducible-list assumption to get `Nodup` of
   `coreFactors.toList.map toPolynomial`; pull back through
   injectivity of `toPolynomial` (since `equiv` is a ring iso) to get
   `coreFactors.toList.Nodup`.
3. Use `List.map_fst_zip` and the length hypothesis to split
   `coreFactors.toList = pre.map fst ++ q :: suf.map fst`. `Nodup`
   then rules out `q` appearing in `suf.map Prod.fst`.
4. Transport the assumed `q ∣ X` divisibility to `Polynomial ℤ`
   (using `toPolynomial_factorPower_foldl` to unfold the suffix
   product), apply `Prime.dvd_prod_iff` and `Prime.dvd_of_dvd_pow` to
   force `toPolynomial q ∣ toPolynomial q_j` for some `q_j` in suf.
5. `Irreducible.associated_of_dvd` and
   `normalize_toPolynomial_of_normalizeFactorSign_id` collapse the
   association to equality of the transported polynomials.
6. Injectivity of the ring equiv finishes: `q = q_j`, contradicting
   step 3.
