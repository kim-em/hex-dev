# #8647 — nullspace / `ZMod64.inv` cost-share measurement

**Accomplished**

- Measured the two cost shares #8647 asked for, on current `main` (commit
  `d0d94fc`), via `RELIFT_PROFILE=prime` under `perf` (60k-sample dwarf flat +
  two fp folded captures, reproduced). Wrote
  `reports/bz-nullspace-inv-cost-shares.md`.
- Findings: generic `Matrix.nullspace` / row-reduction under `fixedSpaceKernel`
  is now ~15-22% (inclusive ~21%, self ~19%), the largest algorithmic share and
  a large rise over the pre-#8633 deg-24 ~8%; `ZMod64.inv`/`extGcd` is ~3% with
  negligible GMP-internal work. Nullspace grew because #8633/#8638 made the
  field arithmetic word-fast while the boxed generic-`Matrix` representation
  (typeclass dispatch, `Array.ofFn`-of-`Array.ofFn`, `List` free-col extraction)
  was untouched.

  measured effect (#8644), and neither share is a #8639 multiply-kernel call
  (nullspace = elimination via contextless `ZMod64.mul`/`add`/`inv`; inv =
  `extGcd`). So the "once #8639 lands" gate is unmet and irrelevant; the
  measurement stands on current `main`.
- Decision per the deliverable: nullspace material -> filed
  https://github.com/kim-em/hex-dev/issues/8669 (de-box the row-reduction into a
  boundary-free `ZMod64`-monomorphic kernel, explicitly not the regressed packed
  kernel of #8634); `ZMod64.inv` (~3%) not material -> recorded and closed, no
  issue.
- Second opinion (Codex) reviewed premise/method/decision; applied its wording
  and attribution-precision fixes (materiality rests on inclusive %, not the
  shared ~60% machinery buckets; softened the deg-24-vs-aggregate ratio; hard
