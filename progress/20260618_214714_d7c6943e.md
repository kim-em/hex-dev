### Payoff

The UInt64 conformance example in `HexArith/Conformance.lean` that
previously needed `grind [HexArith.UInt64.extGcd_spec]` now closes with
bare `grind`, since the component projection lemmas it splits into are
themselves `@[grind =]`.

### Deliberate exclusions

The `let`-destructured combined forms were left `@[simp]`-only and NOT
given `grind`:

- `extGcd_bezout` / `pureIntExtGcd_bezout` / Int / UInt64 `extGcd_bezout`
  (let-bound equation),
- `extGcd_spec` / `pureIntExtGcd_spec` / Int / UInt64 `extGcd_spec` and
  `extGcd_spec_ofNat` (let-bound conjunctions).

`grind =` wants a bare `lhs = rhs`; these wrap their content in a
`let (g,s,t) := …` pattern (and the `_spec` forms are conjunctions). Their
grind-usable content is already carried by the `_fst` / `_bezout_proj` /
`_bezout_gcd` projection lemmas, so adding grind here would be redundant
at best. `extGcd_zero_left_s_ofNat` is a one-off special-case fact, not a
general rewrite, and stays unannotated.
