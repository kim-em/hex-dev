## Current frontier

Design (A) concretized for the proven implementation:

- Keep `scaledRecombinationSmartAux` and its entire proof set untouched.
  Do NOT thread subsets through the scan; instead recover each returned
  piece's tracked base sublist by a mod-p **divisibility filter** (u
  divides the piece's monic-transform image, suitably dilated): r cheap
  FpPoly mods per node, and the filter's boolean result is exactly the
  witness the fresh partition producer consumes.
- Sub-floor rungs need NO coverage proofs (peels are self-certifying via
  `exactQuotient?`; only product-reconstruction soundness needed), so they
  can use a new small capped scan. The floor rung reuses
  `scaledRecombinationSmart` verbatim with its existing coverage stack,
  applied to the piece at its own fresh `LiftData`.
- Non-monic cores: the parent's base factors are mod-p factors of
  `(toMonic parent).monic`; a piece's are factors of `(toMonic piece).monic`
  — related by a mod-p dilation (unit substitution, a ring automorphism up
  to scaling). Needs a small mod-p dilation-transport theory (product
  congruence + Berlekamp-form invariants transported along dilation).
- Ladder: k = 1, 2, 4, ... clamped at the piece's own floor
  `precisionForCoeffBound (defaultFactorCoeffBound (toMonic piece).monic) p`,
  sub-floor scan cap >= 2, fuel = degree for the split recursion.
- Budget: one global candidate budget threaded through all rungs/nodes;
  any exhaustion declines (`none`) as today.
- The Mathlib-layer generalization: the `hselected : toMonicPrimeData? core
  = some primeData` witness threads through ~40 statements in 8 files; the
  fresh producer re-keys the descent/transport chain to the piece's own
  LiftData with the three derived properties (prime / isGoodPrime /
  Berlekamp form) transported along the dilation.
