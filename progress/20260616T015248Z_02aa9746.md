## Accomplished

Issue #7533: proved `associated_of_associated_monicCorrespondent` in
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib/Basic.lean` — the second bridge gating
#7362's `pairwise_disjoint` field. If two factors' monic correspondents
`gf`, `gg` have associated integer images, then the factors `f`, `g`
recovered by `primitivePart (dilate (leadingCoeff core) ·)` are associated
too. Used contrapositively (`¬Associated f g → ¬Associated gf gg`).

Proof, entirely at the `Polynomial ℤ` level:
- `toPolynomial_dilate` embeds the dilation as composition with `C lc * X`;
  `Associated.map` of `Polynomial.compRingHom (C lc * X)` transports the
  hypothesis association to the dilations.
- `toPolynomial_eq_C_content_mul_primitivePart` + the recovery equalities
  `hf`/`hg` rewrite each dilation as `C (content ·) * toPolynomial f/g`.
- A new private helper `associated_of_C_mul_associated` reflects an
  association `C a * F ~ C b * G` (with `F`, `G` primitive, `a, b ≠ 0`) down
  to `F ~ G`: each primitive factor divides its own multiple hence the
  other, and the content scalar is stripped via
  `IsPrimitive.dvd_primPart_iff_dvd` + `isUnit_primPart_C` (the same
  Gauss-content machinery `dilate_recovery` uses), giving divisibility both
  ways → `associated_of_dvd_dvd`.
- Primitivity of `f`, `g` comes from `primitivePart_primitive` (the
  dilations are nonzero, shown by `comp_C_mul_X_eq_zero_iff` over the
  nonzero-divisor `lc`) through `isPrimitive_toPolynomial_of_primitive`.

Hypotheses added beyond the directive's elided `...`: `hlc : leadingCoeff
core ≠ 0`, `hgf : gf ≠ 0`, `hgg : gg ≠ 0` — the minimal conditions making
the dilations (hence the primitive parts) nonzero and the statement true.
The use site (monic `gf`, `gg`; `core` with positive leading coeff)
supplies all three.
