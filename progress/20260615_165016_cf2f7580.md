  `sum_range_hexChoose_sq_le`.

Key gotcha discovered: `bhksCoeffBound` uses `Hex.Nat.choose` (the Mathlib-free
executable binomial in `HexArith/Nat/Prime.lean`), NOT Mathlib's `Nat.choose`.
The two share the Pascal recursion but are distinct declarations, so a bridge
lemma is required before any Mathlib choose fact applies. Also note the honest
height carrier is `L² = (coeffL2NormBound input)²`, not `coeffNormSq input` as
the issue's informal statement said: `L = ceilSqrt(coeffNormSq) ≥ ‖input‖₂`, so
`L² ≥ coeffNormSq`; stating the bound against `coeffNormSq` would be false.

`lake build HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib.BadVector` is green; no new warnings,
no sorry/axiom.

## Current frontier

Feasibility of the real-analysis core (sub-piece 3) is confirmed at the level
of the joint cancellation, with one boundary caveat (see Next step). The
decisive structural fact the prior failed targets (#7440/#7441/#7487) missed:
in the JOINT product `‖core‖₂^(deg aux) · ‖aux‖₂^n` with the SHARP degree
`deg aux ≤ n-1`, the `N^(2n-1)` (where `N = ‖core‖₂`) cancels EXACTLY against
the threshold's `bhksPaperCoeffNormFactorReal = N^(2n-1)`, leaving the
N-independent inequality

    n^(n-1) ≤ 2^(n²+n) · (log N)^n .

This is feasible (the `4^(n²)=2^(2n²)` slack beats `2^(n²-n)` with `2^(n²+n)`
to spare, and reachable cores give `log N ≥ log √2 ≈ 0.347`). The `_factored`
split lemma at `Recovery.lean:5333` cannot do this — it bounds `‖aux‖₂^n` alone
against the fixed aux factor, and that carries an un-cancellable `N^n`.
