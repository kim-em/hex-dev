## Key patterns discovered

- **`@[grind]` default-pattern trap.** `findPivot?_ge_start` (hyp
  `findPivot? … = some pivot` ⊢ `start ≤ pivot.val`) under plain `@[grind]`
  gets a *multi-pattern* `[start ≤ ↑pivot, findPivot? …]` that requires the
  conclusion's `≤` to already exist — so it never fires to *derive* the bound.
  The fix is `@[grind →]` (forward, keyed on the hypothesis). Same for
  `findPivot?_some_ne_zero` and `det_eq_zero_of_singularStep`. Confirmed with a
  `lake env lean` scratch spot-check that all three now close under bare
  `grind`.
- **`findPivot?_eq_none_of_zero` is a harmful candidate.** The issue listed it,
  but its conclusion `findPivot? … = none` makes grind E-match on *every*
  `findPivot?` term and inject an existential case-split from the ∀-premise
  (`hzero`), which derailed the unrelated `findPivot?_ge_start` goal in the
  spot-check. Excluded with that evidence (directive-as-hypothesis: candidate
  found unsound for grind).
- `getEntry_rangeMap₂` rejected by `grind =` (hard error): the function param
  `f` lives only inside the inner `Array.map`, unreachable from the LHS pattern.
  Excluded.
- `let`-wrapped RHS (`stepMatrix_update_eq`) and divisibility-proof-term RHS
  (`exactDiv_eq_divExact`) excluded per the standing eligibility rules.
- The `findPivotAux_*` / `findPivotArrayAux_*` fuelled helpers are left untagged
  as subsumed by their `findPivot?_*` wrappers (avoid duplicate rules); the
  `*_matches` and `getEntry_*_matches` lemmas carry a `∀`-premise grind cannot
  discharge.
