### `toMathlibPolynomial_monic` (no extra hypothesis)

Provable unconditionally. Used `Polynomial.monic_of_natDegree_le_of_coeff_eq_one
(f.size - 1)`: the degree bound from coefficient vanishing above `f.size`, and
`coeff (size-1) = leadingCoeff = 1` via a helper `have hlc` that handles the
degenerate `size = 0` case (both sides `0`) and the `size > 0` case
(`leadingCoeff_eq_coeff_last`). No nontriviality needed: when `ZMod p` is trivial
every polynomial is monic.

### `natDegree_toMathlibPolynomial_eq_basisSize` (premise check: needs Nontrivial)

**Premise was unsound as written.** With no nontriviality hypothesis the claim
fails at `p = 1`: `ZMod 1` is trivial so the transport collapses to `0`
(natDegree `0`) while `basisSize` can be positive (`f = X`). Added the minimal
`[Nontrivial (ZMod p)]`. Proof: monicity + nontriviality force the leading coeff
`1 ≠ 0`, hence `f.size > 0`; then `natDegree = f.size - 1 = basisSize` by
`le_antisymm` of the degree upper bound and `le_natDegree_of_ne_zero` at the
leading coefficient.

### Boundary gotchas hit

`map_zero`/`map_one` do not synthesize for `ZMod64.equiv` (the executable type
lacks Mathlib `MulOneClass`/hom-class instances). Routed all `0`/`1` transport
through the project bridges `coeff_toMathlibPolynomial` + `toZMod_zero`/
`toZMod_one`. The `Zero.zero` vs `OfNat 0` representation split made a `rw
[toZMod_zero]` miss; closed that step with a defeq-tolerant `calc` instead.

### Caller threading

`natDegree_toMathlibPolynomial_eq_basisSize`'s only external caller is the
private `natDegree_toMathlibPolynomial_factorPolys_eq`
(`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib/Basic.lean:1088`), which had no primality in

`Fact (Nat.Prime primeData.p)`, which synthesizes `Nontrivial`. The three
in-module Rabin callers supply `Nontrivial` for free from `[Fact (Nat.Prime p)]`.
