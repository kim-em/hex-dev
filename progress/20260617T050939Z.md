# BHKS early-success soundness audit

## Accomplished

- Reviewed the current BHKS recovery executable surface and Mathlib-side
  recovery/partition wrappers relevant to below-cap first-success branches.
- Confirmed that `bhksRecoverClassified` accepts a branch from nondegenerate
  indicators, per-indicator exact division, and final product equality only;
  it does not check cap precision, true-factor CLD survival, or candidate
  irreducibility.
- Checked the existing proof wrappers: product/divisibility give only an upper
  UFD factor-count bound, while irreducibility still depends on a separate
  count/partition-refinement package.

## Current frontier

Bare below-cap recovery success is not enough to retroactively certify the
fixed-precision tight CLD bound. A coarse nontrivial partition into products of
true factors is compatible with exact division and product equality.

## Next step

If the fast early-exit executable is to remain proof-facing, add an executable
certificate/guard strong enough to rule out coarse classes, or thread explicit
fixed-precision `ForwardRecoveryInputs`/partition data through the soundness
theorem. Product equality alone should not be used as the certificate.

## Blockers

None for this audit. The hard route without an additional guard or explicit
proof certificate appears to have a false premise.
