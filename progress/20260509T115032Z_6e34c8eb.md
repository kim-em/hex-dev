# 20260509T115032Z — HexHensel runHenselLiftChecksum cost-model fix (#2791)

## Accomplished

Resolved issue #2791. `runHenselLiftChecksum` now returns
`consistent with declared complexity` over the full encoded
`(n, k)` ladder including `param=192_064`, and
`HexHensel.done_through` is bumped from 3 to 4.

Root cause was not the cost model nor a missing per-step coefficient
reduction in `Hex.ZPoly.henselLift` (the two hypotheses the issue
floated), but a degenerate fixture: `prepLinearInput` initialised
the Bezout pair to `(s, t) = (0, 1)`, which violates the linear
Hensel precondition `s * gMod + t * hMod ≡ 1 (mod 5)` whenever
`hMod` does not have constant term `1`. With that precondition
violated the iterative lift no longer keeps the corrected `h`
factor bounded in degree: a probe inside this worktree confirmed
that `h.size` grew linearly with `k` under the degenerate seed
(`33 → 65 → 129 → 257 → 385 → ...`) while the same `henselLift`
called from `prepMultifactorLiftInput` (which computes a real
Bezout pair via `normalizedXGCD`) kept `h.size` flat at `33`
across all `k`. The growing `q * hMod` correction product is what
inflated per-call cost from `n²·k` to roughly `n²·k²`.

The fix was to compute the Bezout pair via `normalizedXGCD` inside
`prepLinearInput` and align the salts with `prepMultifactorLiftInput`
(which has already been verified coprime modulo 5 across the full
scientific `n` ladder including `n = 192`; the previous salts
produced `gcd ≠ 1` precisely at `n = 192`, which would have left
that ladder rung degenerate even after the bezout fix).

After the fix, the scientific run at `24a4fca` on `carica`
returns `consistent` for all 9 registered Hex.HenselBench targets,
including `runHenselLiftChecksum` at `cMin=320.073, cMax=396.792,
β=−0.096` over the full `32_004..192_064` ladder. The full export
artefact landed at `reports/bench-results/hex-hensel-24a4fca.json`
(SHA-256 `4633cdcaed2f7d0b056c7eb7b754258c9997f6360427b5a8d28b421e5ae53b49`),
replacing the predecessor PR #2792's
`hex-hensel-60dbf8026826.json`. The `linear-hensel` profile case in
`reports/hex-hensel-performance.md` was also re-recorded at
`24a4fca` to reflect the corrected fixture (per-call wallclock at
`n = 512` roughly doubles from `41.97 ms` to `83.76 ms` because
`s * eMod` is now a real F_5 polynomial multiplication rather than
the trivially-zero short-circuit it was under `s = 0`); the other
three profile cases are unchanged from `60dbf80` because their
fixtures are unaffected. The `runHenselLiftChecksum` §Concerns
entry is removed; §Concerns is now empty.
