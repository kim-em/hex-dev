### Design decision: bundle four fields into the analytic obligation

The issue text suggested deriving `pairwise_disjoint` and
`unique_up_to_associated` from `unique_subset` alone, with a small
helper for `RepresentsIntegerFactorAtLift` invariance under
`Associated`.  However, `RepresentsIntegerFactorAtLift core d factor S`
is defined as a signed `reduceModPow` equality of the scaled lifted
product against `factor`, which is **not** invariant under unit factors
in `Polynomial ℤ` (units there are `±1`; the predicate distinguishes
`f` from `-f`).  So:

* For `pairwise_disjoint`, two non-associated irreducible divisors with
  overlapping representing subsets need an analytic argument (cover at
  the shared index + analytic Hensel uniqueness) that goes beyond
  `unique_subset`.

* For `unique_up_to_associated`, even for associated `f, g`, the
  predicates `RepresentsIntegerFactorAtLift core d f S` and
  `RepresentsIntegerFactorAtLift core d g T` are different in general;
  the conclusion `S = T` is genuinely analytic.

Both fields are therefore bundled into the analytic obligation helper
alongside `cover` and `support_subset_of_dvd_recombinationCandidate`,
yielding a single localised `sorry` for the combined content.  The
constructor itself remains `sorry`-free and structurally derived (apart
from the helper application), preserving the spirit of the issue's
"one localised sorry" fallback.

### Design decision: take `Squarefree (toPolynomial core)` as hypothesis

There is no existing `Squarefree (HexPolyZMathlib.toPolynomial
(normalizeForFactor f).squareFreeCore)` lemma in the codebase
(`HexPolyZ/Basic.lean` proves `SquareFreeRat` over `ℚ[x]`, not Mathlib's
`Squarefree` over `Polynomial ℤ`).  Per the issue's explicit option (a),
the parametric constructor takes `hcore_sqfree` as an explicit
hypothesis; the outer-bound specialisation threads the same hypothesis
at `core := (normalizeForFactor f).squareFreeCore`.  Downstream HO-1
assemblies will supply this from their own square-free-core invariants
(or a future `Mathlib`-level bridge from `SquareFreeRat` to
`Squarefree`, which is out of scope here).
