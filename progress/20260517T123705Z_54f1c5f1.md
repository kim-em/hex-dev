- `S ≤ factors`: from the existing

- `S.prod = d`: from Mathlib's
  `UniqueFactorizationMonoid.prod_normalizedFactors_eq` (which gives
  `(normalizedFactors d).prod = normalize d`) together with
  `normalize d = d`.

Uniqueness: any `S ≤ factors` consists of irreducibles and
normalize-fixed elements (transported through the sub-multiset
relation), so `normalizedFactors S.prod = S.map normalize = S` by
`normalizedFactors_prod_eq`; substituting `S.prod = d` gives
`S = normalizedFactors d`.

Empty-factors branch: `factors.prod = 1`, hence `d ∣ 1`, hence
`IsUnit d`, hence `normalize d = 1`, hence `d = 1` (via
`hd_norm`). Unique submultiset of `0` is `0`, with `0.prod = 1 = d`.
