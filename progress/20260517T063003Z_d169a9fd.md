### D3. Helper reuse surface — consolidation applied

Found a real duplication between the two private helpers in
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib/Basic.lean`:

- `polyProduct_map_liftToZ_congr_factorProduct` (from #4573, ~50 line
  induction) proving `congr (polyProduct (xs.map liftToZ)) (liftToZ
  (factorProduct xs)) p`.
- `modP_polyProduct_liftToZ_eq_factorProduct` (from #4574, ~25 line
  induction) proving `modP p (polyProduct (xs.map liftToZ)) =
  factorProduct xs`.

These say the same thing in two forms. `congr (liftToZ u) z p` is exactly
`modP p z = u` rendered through `Hex.ZPoly.congr_liftToZ_of_modP_eq`
(`HexHensel/Linear.lean:200`). So the `_congr_` helper follows from the
`_eq_` helper in two combinators (`congr_liftToZ_of_modP_eq` + `congr_symm`).

Refactor:
1. Hoisted `modP_polyProduct_liftToZ_eq_factorProduct` to immediately
   precede `polyProduct_map_liftToZ_congr_factorProduct` (it is now the
   shared substrate for both dischargers).
2. Replaced the ~50-line induction in `polyProduct_map_liftToZ_congr_factorProduct`
   with a one-line corollary.
3. Updated docstrings to reflect the shared role and corollary derivation.

Net diff: -33 lines, no signature changes, no public-API impact (both helpers
remain `private`). Build green; warning/`sorry` count unchanged at 10 in the
file.

### D5. `nil` branch of the induction helper

Genuinely needed. Structural induction on `factors : List (FpPoly p)` bottoms
out at the empty list; the recursive call inside `cons g rest` passes through
`rest`, which can itself be empty. The discharger's call-site is
`(berlekampFactor ...).factors` (always nonempty by `_ne_nil` sibling), but
the bridge helper is called at the list level *after* peeling, so the
induction must close at `nil`. Sidestepping it (e.g. by inducting on
`factors.length` with a degenerate-`length=0` short-circuit) would just
relocate the same `polyProduct_empty` + `modP_one` reasoning.

In the refactored form, the `nil` discharge lives entirely inside
`modP_polyProduct_liftToZ_eq_factorProduct` (one `rw [polyProduct_empty]`
and one `exact modP_one p`); the corollary inherits it automatically.
