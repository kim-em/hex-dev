## Measured per-call protocol overhead

Piping 10000 trivial `[[1,0],[0,1]]` inputs through the patched
`svp_verified` on the audit host (median of 5 trials) takes ~110 ms
wall total. After subtracting one-time GHC startup (~22 ms),
steady-state per-call protocol overhead is **~9 µs** — three orders
of magnitude below the previous ~22 ms per-call shell-out shape.

## Caveat documented in PR / docstring (not a blocker for HO-16)

`lean-bench` spawns one fresh `hexlll_bench` child process per
measured repeat of a `setup_fixed_benchmark` target. The
`PersistentComparator` cache is per-process, so each repeat still
pays the one-time ~22 ms GHC start cost. Within one child it
amortises — no per-call `IO.Process.output` or `IO.FS.writeFile`
round-trips — but the wall-time-per-call for the current fixed
`runIsabelle*` targets remains dominated by the GHC start, not the
~9 µs protocol overhead. Achieving the HO-16 verification target
"wall time per call ≪ 10 ms" would additionally require either
restructuring the `runIsabelle*` registrations from
`setup_fixed_benchmark` to a parametric shape (so `autoTune` runs
many inner calls per child) or sharing the driver across child
processes. Both are outside HO-16's stated scope; HO-18 (report
regeneration) is the right place to decide which path to take.

The per-call protocol-overhead figure (deliverable #4) is the
operative number for HO-18's overhead-adjusted ratios per the
post-#3657 SPEC, and that is met decisively.
