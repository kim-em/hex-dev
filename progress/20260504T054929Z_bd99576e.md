# 2026-05-04 05:49Z — feature session, issue #1994

## Accomplished

- Closed #1994 by adding the `hex-berlekamp` python-flint oracle:
  `HexBerlekamp/EmitFixtures.lean` (Lean-side JSONL emitter),
  `scripts/oracle/berlekamp_flint.py` (python-flint cross-check
  driver), `conformance-fixtures/HexBerlekamp/berlekamp.jsonl`
  (committed regression sample), and matching CI steps in
  `oracle-pyflint`.
- Coverage: 18 monic `F_p[x]` polynomials at `p ∈ {5, 7, 11, 13}`
  spanning the three SPEC structural shapes — already-irreducible,
  products of distinct-degree irreducibles, and polynomials with
  repeated factors.  Each emits two `result` records (`rabin`,
  `ddf`), giving 36 cross-checked operations.
- `rabin` cross-check: python-flint reports irreducibility iff
  `nmod_poly.factor()` returns exactly one factor of multiplicity 1
  with degree equal to the input.  Verified Lean's `rabinTest`
  agrees on every fixture.
- `ddf` cross-check: two invariants per case.  Always: `∏ buckets ·
  residual == f` (Lean's bucket-product reconstruction).  For
  square-free `f`: each Lean bucket equals (after monic
  normalisation) the FLINT product of irreducible factors of that
  degree.  Lean's `DensePoly.gcd` over a field is canonical only up
  to a unit, so monic-normalise both sides before comparing.
- `sfd` (square-free decomposition) intentionally not cross-checked
  here.  Lean's executable SFD splits leading-coefficient units
  into explicit constant factors (e.g. for irreducible `x²+2` over
  `F_5` it returns `factors=[(3+4x², 1), (2, 2)]` rather than the
  monic-canonical `[(x²+2, 1)]`), so direct comparison to FLINT's
  square-free factorisation requires a lossy renormalisation.
  `HexPolyFp/Conformance.lean` already self-verifies SFD
  reconstruction; a dedicated SFD oracle is left for follow-up.
- Verified corruption-detection both ways: flipping a `rabin` value
  trips the `rabin` check; perturbing a DDF bucket coefficient
  trips the `ddf-product` reconstruction check.  Both deliberate
  corruptions reverted before commit.
- CI wiring: added two steps to the existing `oracle-pyflint` job
  (`Build hexberlekamp_emit_fixtures`, `Cross-check HexBerlekamp
  Lean emission against committed fixture`, `Run HexBerlekamp
  python-flint oracle`).  Renamed the existing HexPoly steps for
  clarity now that the job exercises two libraries.
- Resolved a one-commit lakefile rebase conflict against #2037
  (HexHensel emit exe), keeping both `hexberlekamp_emit_fixtures`
  and `hexhensel_emit_fixtures` registered.
