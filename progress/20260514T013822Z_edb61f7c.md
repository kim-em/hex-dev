## Accomplished

Closed the two adjacent-swap pivot sorries in `HexGramSchmidt/Update.lean`
called out by issue #3886:

- `gramDet_adjacentSwap_pivot`: the integer division formula
  `gramDet b' k = (gramDet b (k+1) · gramDet b km1 + B²) / gramDet b k`.
- `adjacentSwap_gramDetNumerator_dvd`: the matching divisibility that
  justifies the exact integer division.

Both follow from a new public theorem in `HexGramSchmidt/Int.lean`:
`gramDet_rowSwap_adjacent_pivot_product`, the fraction-free integer
product identity `gramDet b' k · gramDet b k = gramDet b (k+1) · gramDet
b km1 + B²` where `b' = Matrix.rowSwap b km1 k` and `B = (scaledCoeffs
b)[k][km1]`. The proof goes via the rational `gramSchmidtNormProduct`
decomposition: it expresses each `gramDet` as a product of `‖basis i‖²`,
uses Pythagoras for the swapped pivot vector (Gram-Schmidt orthogonality
of `basis k ⊥ basis km1`), and discharges the resulting polynomial
identity over `Rat` with `grind`.

Supporting helpers added in `Int.lean` (all `private`):

- `gramDet_eq_prod_normSq_uncond`: the unconditional rational
  `gramDet = gramSchmidtNormProduct` (the `independent` hypothesis on
  the existing public theorem is not actually used by its proof body).
- `gramDet_subst_val`, `gramSchmidtNormProduct_subst_val`: `Nat`-value
  congruences that bridge the `(km1.val + 1)` form of
  `gramDet_succ_rat` to the `k.val` form requested by the theorem
  statements.
- `intCast_rat_injective_int_eq`: integer injectivity through the
  rational cast.
- `dot_add_left_rat`, `dot_smul_left_rat`: left-distribution / left-
  scalar versions derived from the existing right-side privates plus
  `dot_comm_rat`.
- `normSq_add_smul_orthogonal_rat`: Pythagoras for `curr + μ • prev`
  when `dot curr prev = 0`.
- `gramSchmidtNormProduct_rowSwap_below`: the norm-square product over
  indices `< km1.val` is invariant under swapping rows `km1, k`, via
  `basis_rowSwap_of_before` and `foldl_mul_congr_simple`.
