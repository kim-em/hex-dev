## Accomplished

Re-audited HexLLL's headline report, found two compounding bugs in the comparator-ratio measurement, and landed the SPEC-side response as one PR + five HO issues.

**Audit findings.** The original report's `gating`-goal claim ("Lean is at least as fast as verified Isabelle on shared canonical inputs") was being made on the basis of bottom-rung ratios like "9814× faster on BZ recombination" and "9.21× faster on random-bounded n=30". Re-measurement showed:
- **Bug 1: process-spawn overhead.** Isabelle is wired via per-call subprocess (`HexLLL/Bench.lean:607`); GHC startup + file parse is ~22 ms fixed per call, while Lean is in-process. Small-n ratios measure subprocess invocation, not algorithm.
- **Bug 2: bottom-rung verdict anchoring.** The previous SPEC clause "ratio at the bottom of the parameter ladder" sits at the structurally most flattering rung. The full table shows Lean catching Isabelle around n≈80 on random-bounded and reaching ~1.20× *slower* at n=240; harsh-cubic ratios double per rung step over {15, 30, 45} (0.04 → 0.48 → 1.14), with the trend extrapolating worse. Lean does not actually meet the gating goal at the top of either ladder.

**SPEC PR #3657 — doctrine-only amendment.** Two files:
- `SPEC/benchmarking.md`: §"External comparators" §"Process call" now mandates per-call overhead measurement, prefers persistent-subprocess as the shape when overhead is non-negligible, and demotes per-call process spawn to last resort. §"Headline reports" §"Comparator ratios" is rewritten to require ratios across the full parameter ladder, defines an explicit eligible range (per-call overhead ≤ 50%, per-call wall time ≤ 10 s hard / 1 s soft), mandates enough rungs inside the eligible range to read the trend unambiguously (in-fill rungs between doublings when needed; never past the wallclock ceiling), and evaluates the gating-goal verdict at the largest eligible rung. Adverse trends are audit-found Concerns even when the highest-rung verdict passes. §"Anti-patterns" adds "Comparator process overhead reported as algorithmic difference" and "Bottom-rung-only comparator evaluation".
- `SPEC/Libraries/hex-lll.md`: performance-goal sentence rewritten to evaluate at the largest eligible rung per the new generic rule; new §"Comparator-call protocol" paragraph wires fpLLL and verified Isabelle as persistent subprocesses.
