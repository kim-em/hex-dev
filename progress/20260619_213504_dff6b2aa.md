## Deliverable 3 decision (shared-LHS coefficient pair)

The two coefficient lemmas share the LHS `(toMathlibPolynomial f).coeff n`
with interconvertible RHS (`toZMod` vs `equiv`). I promoted **both**, on
evidence:

- Empirical: `HexBerlekampMathlib.Basic` builds at 3.2s with three
  promotions and 3.3s with all four — within noise of the 3.2s baseline.
  No grind-loop slowdown.
- No loop is structurally possible: neither RHS (`toZMod …` / `equiv …`)
  matches the LHS pattern `(toMathlibPolynomial _).coeff _`, and no reverse
  rule rewrites a RHS form back to the LHS, so grind's E-matching reaches a
  fixed point (the e-graph merges {LHS, toZMod form, equiv form} once).
- `HexModArithMathlib.ZMod64.equiv_apply` (`equiv x = toZMod x`) is
  `@[simp]` but **not** `@[grind =]`, so grind has no other route from the
  `equiv` form to the canonical `toZMod` form. Keeping the second rule is
  therefore the only thing that bridges an `equiv`-phrased goal to the LHS
  for grind — genuine value, not redundancy. The issue's "prefer toZMod,
  drop the other" fallback applies only on a measured slowdown, which did
  not occur.
