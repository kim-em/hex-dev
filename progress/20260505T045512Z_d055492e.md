# Work session: Hex.Nat exponent identity for xPowSubX divisibility

## Accomplished

- Initially claimed #2270 (`xPowSubX_dvd_of_dvd` polynomial divisibility
  chain). On scope assessment found three independent components: a
  natural-number Fermat-style exponent identity, polynomial monomial
  multiplication and geometric-series divisibility, and the combine
  step. Decomposed into #2278 (Nat) and #2279 (polynomial) and skipped
  #2270 with a `Decomposed into ...` breadcrumb.
- Claimed #2278 (label: `feature`).
- Added new file `HexArith/Nat/Pow.lean` housing
  `Hex.Nat.sub_one_dvd_pow_sub_one (x j : Nat) : x - 1 ∣ x ^ j - 1` and
  the headline lemma
  `Hex.Nat.pow_sub_one_dvd_pow_sub_one_of_dvd (p : Nat) {d m : Nat}
  (hdvd : d ∣ m) : p ^ d - 1 ∣ p ^ m - 1`.
- Wired the new module into `HexArith.lean`.
- Proof uses the witness identity `x ^ j = (x - 1) * k + 1` (induction
  on `j`, witness `k' := x * k + 1` at the successor step), avoiding
  the Nat-subtraction-underflow trap by working in the `+ 1`
  formulation. The headline lemma reduces `p^(d*j)` to `(p^d)^j` via
  `Nat.pow_mul` and applies the building-block lemma.
- Edge cases handled cleanly:
  - `x = 0`: case-splits on `j` (with `x^0 = 1` and `x^(j+1) = 0` for
    `j ≥ 0`); both reduce to `0 ∣ 0`.
  - `x = 1`: falls into the `1 ≤ x` branch and goes through the witness
    identity (`x - 1 = 0`, witness vacuous).
  - `d = 0`: `d ∣ m` forces `m = 0`, both sides `0 ∣ 0`.
