## Accomplished

- Claimed #3672 (HO-24): wire FLINT `nmod_poly_hensel_lift_*` as the
  informational external comparator for HexHensel via the shared
  persistent-subprocess driver from HO-20.
- The HO-20 driver already exposes the `nmod_poly_hensel` family
  with `lift_once` and `lift` ops (emulated through `fmpz_poly`
  arithmetic because python-flint does not bind the
  `nmod_poly_hensel_lift_*` C entry points). No driver extension was
  needed for HO-24; the Hex-side wiring consumes the existing ops.
- Wired five FLINT comparator pairs in `HexHensel/Bench.lean`, one
  per Hensel-lift Hex registration:
  `runLinearHenselStepChecksum` ↔ `nmod_poly_hensel.lift_once`,
  `runHenselLiftChecksum` ↔ `nmod_poly_hensel.lift`,
  `runQuadraticHenselStepChecksum` ↔ `nmod_poly_hensel.lift_once`,
  `runMultifactorLiftChecksum` ↔ `nmod_poly_hensel.lift`,
  `runMultifactorLiftQuadraticChecksum` ↔ `nmod_poly_hensel.lift`.
  Each Hex registration is paired with six per-rung
  `setup_fixed_benchmark` registrations (Hex + FLINT) inside the
  driver-safe parameter range.
- Single-step pairs (Linear / Quadratic) use `n ∈ {64, 128, 192,
  256, 384, 512}` at `k = 1` (with `n = 128` swapped for `n = 160`
  on the Quadratic ladder, because the `QuadraticInput` fixture at
  `n = 128` has `gcd(g, h) ≠ 1 (mod 5)` and trips the FLINT driver's
  Bezout precondition).
- Iterated lifters (HenselLift / MultiLift / MultiLiftQ) use the
  encoded ladder `(n, k = 8)` for `n ∈ {32, 64, 96, 128, 192, 256}`.
  The python-emulated FLINT lift cannot reach the Hex parametric
  schedule's `(192, 64)` extreme — empirical measurement shows the
  driver consuming > 12 GB at `(128, 16)` and > 20 GB at
  `(192, 16)`, so the FLINT pairing caps at `k = 8` and the trend
  is read along the `n` axis.
- Regenerated `reports/hex-hensel-performance.md` with the new
  Comparator Ratios section: per-call overhead figure
  (65.8 ms median, 60.6 ms min on `carica`), per-rung Hex / FLINT
  median / raw ratio / overhead-adjusted ratio / eligibility table
  across all five pairings, and a trend narrative for each. Two
  Concern entries surface:
    1. Hex's single-step Hensel lift carries ~25–70× the algorithmic
       wallclock of FLINT's tuned `lift_once` (single Newton step at
       `k = 1` returns in < 5 ms in FLINT versus 83–355 ms in Hex
       at `n = 512`), recorded for orientation rather than as a
       gating verdict.
    2. The python-emulated iterated `lift` runs 3.2–3.8× slower than
       Hex at `(n = 256, k = 8)` across the iterated-lift surfaces,
       reflecting the `fmpz_poly` operand-doubling cost rather than
       an algorithm-level slowdown; the SPEC's `informational`
       classification stands.
- Added the comparator to `libraries.yml: HexHensel.phase4.comparators`.
- The CI install step already includes `python-flint` (HO-22 land)
  and the bench verify step picks up the new fixed-benchmark
  registrations automatically; no workflow edits were needed.
- Local `lake exe hexhensel_bench verify` passes all 69 registrations
  (parametric + 30 paired fixed comparator rungs) in 6.6 s.
