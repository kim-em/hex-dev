### Design decisions

- The bridge's `hcore_entry` hypothesis takes the `entry.1 =
  Hex.normalizeFactorSign raw` shape produced by
  `factorWithBound_entry_mem_exhaustive_branch_raw` directly, rather
  than the `entry.1 = raw` shape used by the existing count-based
  sibling `factorWithBound_exhaustive_branch_entry_core_zpolyIrreducible_of_count`.
  This eliminates the consumer-side step of stripping the sign
  normalisation.
- Deliverable 1 is locked to `B = defaultFactorCoeffBound core`
  because the upstream coverage theorem fixes the precision exponent
  at `precisionForCoeffBound (defaultFactorCoeffBound core)
  primeData.p`. The slow-path consumer (`factor f` at the public
  entry) currently uses `B = defaultFactorCoeffBound f`, not
  `defaultFactorCoeffBound (squareFreeCore)`; bridging the two B's
  is out of scope for #4006 and remains a separable downstream
  composition step.
