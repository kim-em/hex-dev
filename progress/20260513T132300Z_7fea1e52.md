## Current frontier

The Comparator Ratios subsection shows two structurally different
shapes:

1. NTL `add` is dominated by the hex-marshaling round-trip at every
   rung — NTL's underlying `add` kernel is sub-millisecond at every
   fixture size, but the driver passes operands as hex-encoded byte
   strings whose parse cost scales linearly with the polynomial size.
   The `add` ratios are reported for completeness, with a note that
   they are not an audit signal for `GF2Poly.add`. A follow-up HO
   could rewire the driver to accept raw binary frames if the `add`
   comparator becomes load-bearing later.
2. NTL `mul` / `div` / `rem` / `gcd` are bounded by NTL's algorithmic
   cost across the upper rungs. NTL crosses Hex around `n = 64–512`
   (surface-dependent) and runs ~25x (mul), ~110x (div), ~140x (rem),
   ~1000x (gcd) faster than Hex's schoolbook packed-word reduction at
   the top eligible rung of each ladder. The gap accelerates with `n`
   on every surface — NTL's Karatsuba/FFT-tuned mul and subquadratic
   GCD are the structural advantage `SPEC/Libraries/hex-gf2.md`'s
   `informational` rationale named in advance. Recorded as the first
   Concern; the comparator is informational so no gating-goal verdict
   is required.
