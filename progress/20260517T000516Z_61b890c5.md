### Why route 3 over routes 1 and 2

- **Route 1 (direct monotonicity proof).** The classical inequality
  chain `coeffL2NormBound g Ōēż binom(deg(g), ŌīŖdeg(g)/2Ōīŗ) ┬Ę L2NormBound f`
  (for `g ŌłŻ f`) combined with degree monotonicity reduces the
  monotonicity to `binom(deg core, ŌīŖŌĆ”/2Ōīŗ)┬▓ Ōēż binom(deg f, ŌīŖŌĆ”/2Ōīŗ)`,
  which holds asymptotically but can be tight when
  `2┬Ędeg(core) > deg(f)`.  A clean Mathlib-free proof would need the
  Mignotte L2 chain on `Polynomial Ōäż` plus a real-arithmetic step;
  substantial new infrastructure either way.
- **Route 2 (counterexample).** I retraced the prior worker's empirical
  search across degree-3-to-10 examples with both small and large
  coefficient ranges (`(X-100)┬▓(X+100)`, `(XŌü┤+1000)(X-1)┬▓`,
  `(XŌüĖ+10ŌüĄ)(X-1)┬▓`, `(X-1)┬▓(X-2)┬▓(X-3)`, etc.).  In every case
  `defaultFactorCoeffBound core` stayed at least an order of magnitude
  below `defaultFactorCoeffBound f`.  Not a proof of impossibility,
  but consistent with the prior session's report that empirical
  monotonicity holds.
- **Route 3 (abstract-bound refactor).** The crucial observation is
  that `defaultFactorCoeffBound_valid` is keyed to *any* polynomial
  `f` and works for any divisor of that `f` ŌĆö not specifically `core`.
  Since `core ŌłŻ f` after content/X-power/repeated-part normalisation,
  factors of `core` are factors of `f`, and `defaultFactorCoeffBound_valid f`
  gives them the outer-bound coefficient validity directly.  The
  precision-using theorems all bottom out at
  `centeredLiftPoly_eq_of_reduceModPow_eq`, which already accepts an
  abstract bound; only the intermediate wrappers hard-code
  `defaultFactorCoeffBound core`.  Re-parameterising those wrappers is
  a mechanical refactor.
