# Session 1e840648 — feature work on `HexPolyFp.Quotient`

## Accomplished

Closes #2404 (HexPolyFp: prove nonzero quotient multiplication permutes
nonzeroElements).

In `HexPolyFp/Quotient.lean`:

- Added `Quotient.mul_zero` and `Quotient.zero_mul` (Mathlib-free,
  computed from `FpPoly.mul_zero` and the canonical zero representative).
- Added `Quotient.mul_left_ne_zero_of_ne_zero`: under a monic irreducible
  positive-degree modulus, the product of two nonzero quotient elements is
  nonzero. Proof multiplies the assumed-zero product on the left by `a⁻¹`
  and uses `inv_mul_cancel`/`mul_assoc`/`mul_zero` to derive `b = 0`.
- Added `Quotient.mul_left_injective`: for `a ≠ 0`, the map `b ↦ a * b` is
  injective on the entire quotient.
- Added `Quotient.nonzeroElements_map_mul_left_perm`: the deliverable
  permutation theorem that `nonzeroElements.map (a *)` is a permutation
  of `nonzeroElements`.
- Added a private helper `perm_of_nodup_mem_iff` showing two `Nodup` lists
  with identical membership predicates are permutations. Implementation by
  recursion on the first list using `List.append_of_mem` and
  `List.perm_middle`. This avoids needing `BEq`/`LawfulBEq` instances on
  `Quotient` (which would otherwise be needed by `List.perm_iff_count`).
