### Proof strategy

The injectivity route is mod-`p` preservation rather than pairwise
coprimality:

1. Each output `arr[i]` of `multifactorLiftQuadratic` reduces mod `p`
   to `FpPoly.liftToZ (factorsModP[i])` (the new
   `_each_congr_mod_base` lemma).
2. If `arr[i] = arr[j]` as integer polynomials, taking `modP p` of
   both sides through their respective congruences gives
   `FpPoly.liftToZ factorsModP[i] ≡ FpPoly.liftToZ factorsModP[j] mod p`.
3. Applying `ZPoly.modP_eq_of_congr` and then `FpPoly.modP_liftToZ`
   collapses the lifts back to `factorsModP[i] = factorsModP[j]`.
4. `List.Nodup.getElem_inj_iff` on `factorsModP.toList.Nodup` then
   forces `i = j`.

This avoids pairwise coprimality and positive natDegree entirely;
degenerate "unit lifted factor" cases are excluded by `Nodup` rather
than by degree. The trade-off is that `Nodup` is a consumer-supplied
hypothesis rather than discharged here.

### Hypothesis trade-off

The umbrella takes `factorsModP.toList.Nodup` as an explicit
hypothesis. The issue body permitted threading this through ("Binders
may be adjusted as proof requires; the exact set of hypotheses should
match … plus whatever extra mod-p / lift facts the proof genuinely
needs (e.g. `factorsModP` itself being `Nodup`)"). The issue body
asserted this was already a `choosePrimeData` invariant, but a search
through `HexBerlekampZassenhaus/Basic.lean:1560-1800` did not find a
stated `Nodup` lemma for `data.factorsModP`. The current
`factorsModPBerlekampForm` invariant only equates the array to
`berlekampFactor.factors.toArray`; no `Nodup` is exposed on that
output. Threading the hypothesis through preserves the umbrella's
intended discharge route while leaving the Berlekamp-side discharge
as a separable follow-up issue.
