## Accomplished

Added a strengthened rest predicate `LiftedFactorSubsetPartition` to
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib/Basic.lean`, extending
`HenselSubsetCorrespondenceRest` with four extra structural fields:

- `target_squarefree`: `Squarefree (toPolynomial target)`. Carries the
  square-free information that the doc-comment on
  `henselSubsetCorrespondenceRest_transport_of_disjoint` flagged as
  needed "from later coverage proofs".
- `cover`: every remaining index `i ∈ J` lies in *some* irreducible
  divisor's representing subset. (Strengthened from the issue's
  `cover_at_min` so the transport proof can apply it to
  `(J \ S).min'`; `cover_at_min` is exposed as a corollary.)
- `pairwise_disjoint`: representing subsets of non-associated
  irreducible divisors of `target` (within `J`) are disjoint.
- `unique_up_to_associated`: representing subsets of associated
  irreducible divisors of `target` (within `J`) are equal. This is the
  generalisation of `HenselSubsetCorrespondenceRest.unique_subset`
  needed for the cover case where the witness factor lands in the
  emitted subset's associated class.

Proved `liftedFactorSubsetPartition_transport`: the predicate
transports through one emitted recombination factor, mirroring
`henselSubsetCorrespondenceRest_transport_of_disjoint`.

- The disjointness obligation of `transport_of_disjoint` is discharged
  using `pairwise_disjoint`: for an irreducible `factor ∣ quotient`,
  `Associated factor emitted` would force
  `(toPolynomial emitted)^2 ∣ toPolynomial target`, contradicting
  `target_squarefree`.
- `Squarefree (toPolynomial quotient)` follows from
  `Squarefree.squarefree_of_dvd` against the parent's square-freeness.
- The cover field transports by case analysis on whether the witness
  factor `f` from the parent's cover is associated to `emitted`. The
  associated case is contradictory (uniqueness gives `T = S`, but
  `i ∈ T` and `i ∉ S`); the non-associated case uses primality in the
  UFD `Polynomial ℤ` to lift `f ∣ target` to `f ∣ quotient`, then
  `pairwise_disjoint` to disjoint-bound the rep subset.
- `pairwise_disjoint` and `unique_up_to_associated` transport
  trivially because `factor ∣ quotient → factor ∣ target` and the
  subsets remain in `J`.

Initial-state derivation from
`HenselSubsetCorrespondenceHypotheses`-plus-square-free is intentionally
deferred per the issue's Deliverable 3.
