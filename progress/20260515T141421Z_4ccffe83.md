## Signature changes

`checkIrreducibleCert_sound` and `checkIrreducibleCert_sound_zpoly`
now take three additional hypotheses:

1. `hprime : ∀ primeData ∈ cert.perPrime.toList, Nat.Prime primeData.p`
   — primality of every certificate prime block. Needed for Rabin
   irreducibility lifting and for `(ZMod p)[X]` UFD reasoning.
2. `hprim : (HexPolyZMathlib.toPolynomial f).IsPrimitive` — rules out
   non-unit integer-constant factor counterexamples that the recorded
   degree obstructions cannot detect.
3. `hpos : 0 < (HexPolyZMathlib.toPolynomial f).natDegree` — rules
   out unit-constant inputs for which the executable check is vacuous.

The cert structure itself does not record primality of `p` or
primitivity of `f`; these have to be supplied by downstream consumers
that know the cert's intended origin. Adding the hypotheses is the
minimal-impact way to make the soundness theorem provable without
extending the executable checker.

## Proof sketch

Given `f = a * b` in `ℤ[X]` with both non-units, pick the smaller of
`a` and `b` (call it `c`); primitivity forces `c.natDegree ≥ 1` and
`c.natDegree ≤ F.natDegree / 2`. The certificate's
`checkIrreducibleCert_obstructs_candidate_degrees` then yields an
obstruction at `c.natDegree`, which selects a `primeData` block with
`hasSubsetDegree c.natDegree = false`. Reduce mod
`p = primeData.p`: Bridge A preserves `natDegree (c.map _) = c.natDegree`
via the good-prime leading-coefficient identity; Bridge B identifies
`F.map _ = (factorPolys.toList.map toMathlibPolynomial).prod`; the
nested Rabin certificates (via #4223 and `PrimeModulus` constructed
from `hprime`) make each factor irreducible; Bridge C then says
`c.natDegree` is a sub-multiset sum of the recorded factor degrees.
The subset-sum spec lemma converts this into `hasSubsetDegree
c.natDegree = true`, contradicting the obstruction.
