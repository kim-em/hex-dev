## Current frontier

The forward `W <= L'` cut argument can be stated at a fixed valid lift precision
provided the true-factor CLD lattice vectors exist and meet the same norm bound.
However, irreducibility of an arbitrary successful below-cap branch should not
depend on proving cap-level bad-vector separation at that branch; the cleaner
target is a BHKS Lemma 3.4-style verification certificate theorem using
`W <= L'`, exact division, and the final product check.

## Next step

`bhksRecover?`/`factorFastCoreWithBound` success witness, with assumptions for
valid lift data, fixed-precision `W <= L'`, per-candidate exact-division facts,
and final product equality. Keep cap separation only for the existence theorem
showing the schedule eventually has a successful branch.

## Blockers

None for this analysis. A proof that omits fixed-precision `W <= L'` would need
a separate argument that successful class indicators refine true irreducible
supports; product equality alone is not enough.
