## Rationale for decomposition (not implementation)

Implementing `bareissNoPivot_eq_det` requires:

1. The Leibniz-formula bridge (`det_eq`): a permutation-sum reindex
   between `Hex.Matrix.permutationVectors n` and `Equiv.Perm (Fin n)`,
   plus matching `Hex.Matrix.detSign` (inversion-count parity) to
   `Equiv.Perm.sign`. ≈100-200 lines.
2. The submatrix bridges: lift `borderedMinor` and `leadingPrefix` into
   Mathlib `Matrix.submatrix` form, then transfer Mathlib's
   `desnanot_jacobi` to a Hex-form recurrence matching `hexact` in
   `stepMatrix_borderedMinor_update`. ≈100-200 lines.
3. The bordered-minor invariant: induction on the `noPivotLoop` step
   carrying the bordered-minor entry equality through. ≈200-300 lines.
4. The capstone composition. ≈50 lines.

Total ≈ 450-750 lines across four logically distinct phases. Each
phase has a clear contract and is independently reviewable; bundling
them into a single PR would be far harder to land than four sequential
narrow PRs.
