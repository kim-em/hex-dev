## Accomplished

Audited HexLLL's Phase-4 claim, found three concrete gaps (identity-basis end-to-end bench, no external comparator wired, no profiling ever recorded), and landed the SPEC-side response as two PRs and nine `human-oversight` issues.

**SPEC PR #1 — doctrine** (https://github.com/kim-em/hex/pull/2687):
- `SPEC/benchmarking.md`: new §"Headline reports" mandating `reports/<lib>-performance.md` with five subsections and artefact traceability; comparator classification `gating` vs `informational` replacing the conflicting `scheduled-only` allowance for required comparators; new §"Comparator naming" pointing at structured `libraries.yml: phase4` metadata; new §"The Attribution rule" (hard, not discretionary) ensuring every dominant profiled cost maps to a registered bench target; two new anti-patterns (best-case-input-as-sole-evidence; dominant-cost-unattributed); profiling moved from "out of scope" to "in scope" with cross-link to the new SPEC/profiling.md.
- `SPEC/profiling.md` (new, ~150 lines): samply + symbolicate + `Lean.Compiler.NameDemangling` workflow; coverage requirement of one case per `phase4.input_families`; required leaf-cost categorisation across {own code, GMP, allocation, Lean runtime}; reproducibility envelope.
- `PLAN/Phase4.md`: new deliverables 6 (profile coverage) and 7 (headline report); new exit criteria for gating-comparator wiring, empty-Concerns, and the Attribution rule; new §"Audit reset" subsection queueing re-evaluation of every library currently at `done_through ≥ 4`.
- `PLAN/Conventions.md`: extended §"Bench-found and conformance-found issues" to §"Bench-found, conformance-found, and audit-found issues" with the new audit-found case and resolution rule.
- `libraries.yml`: documented optional `phase4` schema (comparators with `gating`/`informational` class; named input families) in the header comment.

**SPEC PR #2 — HexLLL specifics** (https://github.com/kim-em/hex/pull/2688, base-branched on PR #1):
- `SPEC/Libraries/hex-lll.md`: replaced the vague §264 "the kind of basis matrix BZ recombination produces" wording with three named input families (`bz-recombination`, `random-bounded`, `harsh-cubic`) including a swap-firing requirement for `random-bounded`; named fpLLL via fpylll (informational, structural fp-vs-integer difference) and verified Isabelle LLL Haskell extraction from Zenodo 2636367 (gating, the only other formally-verified LLL); registered `LLLState.ofBasis` as its own bench target per the new Attribution rule; stated performance goal "Lean LLL is at least as fast as verified Isabelle on shared canonical inputs".
- `libraries.yml`: populated `HexLLL.phase4` block with the structured form of the comparator and input-family metadata.
