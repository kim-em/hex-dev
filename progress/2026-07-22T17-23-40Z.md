# HexManual chapter for hex-roots

## Accomplished

Wrote `HexManual/Chapters/HexRoots.lean`, the Verso reference chapter for
`HexRoots` (certified complex-root isolation), modeled on the existing
`HexRealRoots` chapter. Sections: introduction (atoms vs clusters, the two
atom certificate forms), the `Hex.isolate` entry point, a worked example built
on the smallest-Pisot polynomial `x³ − x − 1`, what the certificate proves
(`isolate_sound`, `DyadicRootIsolation.root`), refining a root, how the
certificate is checked (exact dyadic `nkWitness`), and cross-references.

The Pisot material reuses `HexRootsMathlib.Examples` (`pisot`, `pisot_simple`,
`isolate_pisot`, `pisot_property`) via `{docstring}` directives plus one live
`example`, and points at the runnable `lake exe hexroots_demo`.

Wired into `HexManual.lean`: added the `import` and an `{include 2 ...}` under
"Draft sections for unreleased libraries", right after `HexRealRoots`.

## Current frontier

`lake build HexManual.Chapters.HexRoots`, `lake build HexManual`, and
`lake exe hexmanual` all succeed. Rendered page confirmed to contain the Pisot
worked example, the demo pointer, and the pulled-in docstrings; all `{ref}`
cross-references (`hex-poly-z`, `hex-real-roots`, `hex-roots`) resolve.
