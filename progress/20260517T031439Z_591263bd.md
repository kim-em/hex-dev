### Signature choice

The issue body offered an optional `choosePrimeData?`-selection variant.  The
shape above (`primeData` as a parameter, plus `hform`/`hgood`/`hcore_pos`
triple) was retained verbatim because:

* it composes cleanly with `Hex.choosePrimeData?_factorsModP_berlekamp_form`
  and `Hex.choosePrimeData?_isGoodPrime` at the eventual consumer site — the
  caller passes the selected `primeData` and the three projection lemmas
  applied to it;
* it preserves parity with `_of_choosePrimeData` (same first nine parameters,
  in the same order), making the diff vs. the predecessor easy to audit;
* the consumer (slow-path arm of `factor_irreducible_of_nonUnit`, #4170) has
  not yet been written, so committing to a `choosePrimeData?`-selection shape
  prematurely could lock in a constraint that does not match what `#4170`
  ultimately needs.

The docstring of the new theorem points at the three discharge lemmas
(`_factorsModP_berlekamp_form`, `_isGoodPrime`, and the per-call
`hcore_pos`) so consumers know how to assemble the inputs.

### Existing umbrella untouched

`henselLiftData_liftedFactor_natDegree_pos_of_choosePrimeData` (line 4486)
is unchanged.  Any consumer that already constructs `hfactors_natDegree_pos`
by hand continues to use that umbrella; the new umbrella composes with it
rather than replacing it.
