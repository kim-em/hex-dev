## Accomplished

Closed #4854 by adding `Hex.GramSchmidt.Int.bareiss_scaledCoeffMatrix_rowSwap_above_curr`
in `HexGramSchmidtMathlib/Update.lean`. For `i > k`, with
`b' = rowSwap b (prevRow k hk) k`, the executable Bareiss determinant
of the scaled-coefficient Cramer minor
`scaledCoeffMatrix b' i k hki` times the pivot Gram determinant `d_k`
equals the integer numerator

  `d_{k+1} · nu[i][km1] - B · nu[i][k]`

(with `B = nu[k][km1]`).

The proof mirrors the above-prev structure from #4871 but routes
through two new public helpers added to `HexGramSchmidtMathlib/Int.lean`:

* `dot_basis_rowSwap_curr_prev_eq_normSq` — the `D = ||u_k||²`
  identity, where `D = dot ((basis b').row k) ((basis b).row km1)`.
  Both sides equal `dot u_k (cast b.row km1) = dot u_k (cast b'.row k)`
  (the cast vectors agree because `b'.row k = b.row km1`); the
  prefixCombination remainders vanish on each side by
  `basis_rowSwap_of_before` + basis orthogonality of `b'`.
* `dot_basis_rowSwap_curr_castRow_eq` — for `i > k`,
  `dot u_k (cast b.row i) = D · (c[i][km1] - μ · c[i][k])` with
  `μ = c[k][km1]`. Linearises `cast b.row i` via `basis_decomposition`,
  pushes the dot product through `dot_prefixCombination_right_rat`,
  and isolates the `q = km1` and `q = k` terms via a new private
  `foldl_finRange_isolate_two_rat`. The `q = k` orthogonality
  `dot u_k curr = -μ · D` follows from
  `u_k ⊥ basis b'.row km1 = curr + μ·prev`.

The main theorem combines `(Nk' = D)` and the Cramer-style dot
product expansion to derive
`c'[i][k] · Nk' = Nk' · (c[i][km1] - μ · c[i][k])`, then scales by
`G · Nkm1'` and uses the d_{k+1} permutation invariance
`G · Nkm1' · Nk' = G · Nkm1 · Nk` (from `gramDet_adjacentSwap_of_ne`
at `t = k+1 ≠ k` plus `gramSchmidtNormProduct_rowSwap_below`) to
get `nu'[i][k] = d_{k+1} · (c[i][km1] - μ · c[i][k])`. Multiplied by
`d_k` and unfolded via the standard scaledCoeffs/B/μ rationalisations,
`linear_combination` closes the Int↔Rat equation.

The hypothesis `hdet : gramDet b k.val ... ≠ 0` is kept in the
signature for parity with the issue spec but is not needed by the
rational proof (which goes through unconditionally); it is named
`_hdet` to silence the linter.
