## Accomplished

Claimed #4434 and added the `PrimeChoiceData`-shaped wrapper for the
quadratic multifactor lift invariant.

The implementation adds in `HexBerlekampZassenhaus/Basic.lean`:

- `Hex.ZPoly.QuadraticMultifactorLiftInvariant_of_choosePrimeData`, a
  thin wrapper around #4433's
  `Hex.ZPoly.quadraticMultifactorLiftInvariant_of_factorsModP` that
  threads the invariant through `primeData : Hex.PrimeChoiceData`
  instead of separate `(p, factors)` parameters.

The wrapper hypotheses are:

- `hp_prime : Nat.Prime primeData.p` (used locally to derive
  `ZMod64.PrimeModulus primeData.p`).
- `hp : 1 < primeData.p`, `hB : 1 ≤ B`.
- `hcore_monic : DensePoly.Monic core`.
- `hfactors_monic` over `primeData.factorsModP` as an `Array`.
- `hproduct_mod_p` expressed against the `Array.polyProduct` of the
  lifted array `primeData.factorsModP.map FpPoly.liftToZ`.
- `hcoprime : QuadraticMultifactorCoprimeSplits primeData.p
  primeData.factorsModP.toList`.
- `hnonempty : primeData.factorsModP.toList ≠ []`.

The conclusion lands at
`QuadraticMultifactorLiftInvariant primeData.p B core
(primeData.factorsModP.map FpPoly.liftToZ).toList`, exactly matching
the `hprime_invariant` hypothesis already consumed by
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib.henselLiftData_liftedFactor_monic`.
