## Decisions / patterns

- Statement uses `liftedRecoveryCandidate core d T` (dilated, non-monic
  coordinate), not the monic-coordinate `liftedFactorProductCandidate d T`
  from the issue's sketch shape. Reason: the represented factor `f` is a
  *non-monic* integer factor; only `liftedRecoveryCandidate` (dilation by
  `leadingCoeff core`) lives in `f`'s coordinate system. This matches the
  existing (circular) wrapper
  `representingSubset_subset_of_dvd_liftedRecoveryCandidate_of_primitive_pos_lc_core`
  and the residual-scope comment on #7477. On monic cores the two candidates
  coincide (`liftedRecoveryCandidate.eq_productCandidate_of_lc_one`).
- Route: exact recovery (`RecoveredAtLift.candidate_eq_of_monic_dvd`) gives
  `liftedRecoveryCandidate core d S = f`, so the candidate over `S` divides
  that over `T`; strip `normalizeFactorSign` (associate); reflect the
  dilation over `ℚ`; descend the centred-lift congruence to mod-`p`; feed
  the landed clean half
  `toMonicLiftData_subset_of_liftedFactorProduct_map_dvd` (#7483).
- `p ∤ leadingCoeff core` is NOT available from `toMonicPrimeData?`; the
  proof avoids needing it by reflecting divisibility through the dilation
  over `ℚ` (where `leadingCoeff core ≠ 0` suffices) *before* reducing mod
  `p`.
- Lean gotcha: do not `set d := toMonicLiftData …`. The let-abstraction made
  every `LiftData`-projection defeq check unfold `d` and renamed the
  dependent `S`/`T`, blowing past the `whnf` heartbeat limit. Writing the
  `toMonicLiftData` term explicitly keeps the defeqs syntactic. `.p`/`.k`
  projections are proved via `unfold … ; exact henselLiftData_p/_k`, never
  by `simp [toMonicLiftData]` (which forces the expensive `liftedFactors`
  field) nor bare `rfl`.
