# Phase 6: REDC carry-chain stated over `UInt64.mulFull` pair (issue #6169)

## Accomplished

- Restated `redc_u_spec` in `HexArith/Montgomery/Redc.lean` over the
  `(mhi, mlo)` pair returned by `UInt64.mulFull m p` instead of the split
  `(UInt64.mulHi m p, m * p)` view. Proof bridges back to the split form
  via a single `rw [UInt64.mulFull_eq_mulHi_mul]` and reuses the existing
  carry-chain reasoning, so no new private substrate is introduced.
- Refactored `redc_sub_spec` to consume the `mulFull`-shaped `redc_u_spec`
  directly: removed the local `have hfull` introduction and the
  intermediate `rw [hfull]` step that previously rewrote the executable's
  `match UInt64.mulFull m p` back into the split call. The proof now
  cases on `UInt64.mulFull m p` once and threads `mhi` / `mlo` through the
  carry-chain analysis.

## Current frontier

- `redc_u_spec` is the only `mulFull`-pair carry-chain lemma in the REDC
  surface; no consumer in the codebase still needs the split-call view, so
  no compatibility bridge theorem was added. Callers wanting the split
  view can apply `UInt64.mulFull_eq_mulHi_mul` themselves.
- The executable definition of `redc` is unchanged and `toNat_redc`,
  `redc_lt`, `redc_mul_word_mod` continue to compose cleanly on top of
  `redc_sub_spec`.
