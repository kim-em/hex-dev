## Decisions

- gcd canonicalisation. Lean's `DensePoly.gcd` returns an associate
  of the gcd; I monic-normalise (divide by leading coefficient) on
  the Lean side before emission so the comparison against
  python-flint's already-monic `nmod_poly.gcd` is direct equality.
- Square-free canonicalisation. Lean's `squareFreeDecomposition`
  emits factors that may be constant or non-monic; I hoist constants
  and leading coefficients into the unit, sort the remaining monic
  factors lexicographically, and serialise as
  `[unit, [[coeffs, mult], ...]]`. The Python side applies the same
  hoisting (no-op for python-flint's already-monic factors) and
  sort, then compares.
- Monic Frobenius modulus. `randomMonicPoly` is built as
  `(coeffs of degrees 0..deg-1).push 1` so its `back?` is provably
  `some 1`; this makes `Monic` discharge by `simp` without `sorry`.
- Coefficient generator. A lightweight three-prime mixing function
  `(seed * 73856093 + i * 19349663 + 83492791) mod 2^31-1` keeps the
  fixtures deterministic. The cycle-of-five visible in the F_5
  output is harmless — the oracle still verifies algorithm
  agreement on whatever inputs Lean emits.
