## Accomplished

Oriented on `HexPoly/Euclid.lean` McCoy/content infrastructure and the
chain history (#3383 → #3408 → #3434 → #3440 with helpers in open PR #3442).
Verified that the finite McCoy row lemma cannot be closed by another pure
grid descent: the cells at equal Manhattan distance from the corner
`(bound, k)` form a same-diagonal cycle with one underdetermined equation
per level, so the "larger left" obligation that `finiteToeplitzMcCoyRow_of_larger_left_products`
consumes is provably unreachable from row-only descent.

The algebraic invariant that closes the cycle is the divisibility form
of Gauss's lemma over `Int`: `d ∣ each (p * q).coeff` implies
`d ∣ contentNat p * contentNat q`. With this in hand, truncating
`pCoeff[0..bound]` and `qCoeff[0..k]` to `DensePoly Int` packages closes
the McCoy row in a trivial Bezout step (`contentNat` divides each coefficient,
so `contentNat p * contentNat q ∣ pCoeff i * qCoeff k`).

That divisibility-form Gauss is not currently in `HexPoly/Euclid.lean`. The
only multiplicativity-adjacent results are `content_mul_primitivePart`
(definition) and `primitivePart_primitive` (content one for primitive part).
The "hard direction" — `cont (p * q) ∣ cont p * cont q`, equivalent to
"product of primitive polynomials is primitive" — is the missing prerequisite.

Created sub-issue #3452 capturing exactly that Gauss prerequisite (with
two suggested proof routes: prime-by-prime via CRT, or direct Bezout
combined with primitive part), and skipped #3440 with a `Decomposed into
#3452` breadcrumb and analysis comment on the parent.
