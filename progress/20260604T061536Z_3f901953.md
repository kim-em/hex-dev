## Current frontier

The path from `bareissGramCanonicalCoeff` to a concrete `StepWitness b`
factors through one new piece of operational infrastructure (the augmented
matrix and its bordered-minor identification, #6526) and one composition
piece (Bareiss-Desnanot exact-division on that augmented matrix, #6527).

The key insight motivating the augmented-matrix shape: the Bareiss step on
canonical coefficients is identical in shape to the Bareiss step on matrix
entries, so a single application of `noPivotLoop` on an `(n+1) × (n+1)`
matrix `[gramMatrix b | δ_a^col]` produces both the gram-block evolution
*and* the canonical-coefficient evolution simultaneously. The matrix-side
exact-division lemma `bareissExactDiv_borderedMinor_of_mul_eq` then yields
the witness divisibility for free, without re-deriving Desnanot-Jacobi on
the coefficient side.
