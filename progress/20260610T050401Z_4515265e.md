- `trialDivisionCandidatesUpTo_split_at_degree` — degree-ordered prefix
  split of the candidate enumeration: any threshold `d_split`
  partitions the candidate list into a prefix containing only
  candidates of degree `≤ d_split` and a suffix containing only
  candidates of degree `> d_split`. Proved by reducing to
  `List.range_add` and `List.range'_eq_map_range`.
- `trialDivisionPeel_residual_irreducible` — the peel residual is
  irreducible whenever the running target is a primitive, square-free
  divisor of the original core with positive leading coefficient and
  the trial candidates exhaust every bounded positive-leading
  positive-degree divisor of the core. Sign-normalize the smaller
  factor of an assumed nontrivial decomposition to a positive-leading
  `q ∣ residual`; either `q` is emitted (so `q * q ∣ target ∣ core`
  contradicts `square_not_dvd_of_squareFreeRat`) or `q` is not emitted
  (so `no_missed_unemitted` contradicts `q ∣ residual` via
  `exactQuotient?_eq_some_of_pos_lc_pos_degree_mul_eq`).
- `trialDivisionPeel_factor_irreducible` — every factor emitted by the
  candidate peel is irreducible.  Splits the candidate list at
  `q.degree?.getD 0`, argues `f_i ∈ sufFactors` (since pre-peel
  factors all have degree `≤ d_split < f_i.degree`), uses the suf-peel
  product invariant to derive `q ∣ mid`, applies
  `no_missed_unemitted` on the pre-peel to force `q` into
  `preFactors`, and then closes via `q * f_i ∣ polyProduct peel.1 ∣
  target ∣ core` combined with `q ∣ f_i`.
- `exhaustiveIntegerTrialCoreFactorsWithBound_factor_irreducible` —
  public assembly covering all three emitted-factor families
