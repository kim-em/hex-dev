### Confluence fix

`GFq` is a reducible `abbrev` over `GFqField.FiniteField (conwayPoly …)`, so
`FiniteField.fintype_card` fires through it and left
`Fintype.card (GFq p n h)` at `p ^ degree (conwayPoly p n h)` — the `p ^ n`
form was unreachable because no simp lemma characterised the Conway
polynomial's degree (`modulus_degree` is phrased in `GFq.modulus` form, which
`modulus_eq_conway` rewrites away before it can fire). Added
`conwayPoly_degree` (the `conwayPoly`-phrased twin of `modulus_degree`,
proved from it by defeq) so the chain completes to `p ^ n`. Verified with a
throwaway `example … := by simp` that `Fintype.card (GFq p n h) = p ^ n` now
closes by `simp` alone.

`GFq.fintype_card`'s proof simplified from `simpa … using
(FiniteField.fintype_card …)` to plain `simp [Hex.GFq, Hex.GFq.modulus]` now
that `FiniteField.fintype_card` is `@[simp]` (the `unnecessarySimpa` linter
flagged the old form).
