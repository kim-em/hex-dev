## Descoped: bang forms (`irreducibility!` / `factor_poly!`)

The planned kernel-`decide` fallback through
`Hex.ZPoly.instDecidableIrreducible` is structurally infeasible, not just
slow: kernel reduction of `decide (Hex.ZPoly.Irreducible f)` hard-sticks
even for `x²+1` and even with `import all` of *every* module file in
`HexArith`/`HexModArith`/`HexPoly`/`HexPolyZ`/`HexPolyFp`/`HexBerlekamp`/
`HexBerlekampZassenhaus` plus the relevant `Init.Data.Array.*` modules.
Probing with `whnf` at `.all` transparency traces the sticks to non-exposed
bodies the module system never surfaces to the defeq checker: core-private
`Array` loop workers (`_private.Init.Data.Array.Basic.0.Array.ofFn.go`,
`Array.mapM.map`, `Array.anyM.loop` — `@[irreducible]`,
`WellFounded.Nat.fix`-compiled) and project privates on the factorize path
(e.g. `_private.HexBerlekamp.Factor.0.Hex.Berlekamp.splitWithWitnesses?`).
Re-running the factorizer in the kernel would need an exposure sweep across
the entire executable closure *and* core Init changes — a separate
substrate project, and contrary to the tree's "factorizer never reaches the
kernel" design. The decline diagnostic and SPEC now name this gap honestly
instead of advertising a fallback that cannot elaborate.
