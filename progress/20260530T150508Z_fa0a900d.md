# HexGramSchmidt Phase 5: Mathlib-free singular helper for #5668

Session UUID prefix: `fa0a900d`

## Accomplished

Added private singular helper
`leadingPrefix_gram_bareiss_toNat_eq_zero_of_noPivot_singular` in
`HexGramSchmidt/Int.lean`, the Mathlib-free counterpart of
`leadingPrefix_gram_bareiss_eq_zero_of_singularStep_lt` from the bridge
layer. The helper proves that if the no-pivot Bareiss pass on the full
Gram matrix records a singular step at index `s` after `r` iterations,
then `(Matrix.bareiss (Matrix.leadingPrefix (Matrix.gramMatrix b) (r+1)
(Nat.succ_le_of_lt hr))).toNat = 0`. It is parametric in `hentry_regular`
(the same closed-row-invariant entry equation hypothesis consumed by
`leadingPrefix_gram_zero_pivot_column_zero_of_singular_step`).

The proof composes:

1. `noPivotLoop_prefix_state_at_singular` (ported from HexGramSchmidtMathlib)
   — extracts the partial-pass state at the singular trigger from a
   later-pass singular record.
2. `noPivotLoop_extends_singularStep` (ported from HexGramSchmidtMathlib)
   — the singular fixed point of the no-pivot loop.
3. `leadingPrefix_gram_zero_pivot_column_zero_of_singular_step` (already
   in `HexGramSchmidt/Int.lean` from #5828) — column-zero suffix at the
   triggering step.
4. `noPivotLoop_sync_leadingPrefix_aux` (already there) — sync between
   the full Gram and the leading-prefix trajectories at every fuel
   step that still fits inside the prefix.
5. `Matrix.findPivot?_eq_none_of_zero` — translates column-zero to a
   no-replacement-pivot witness.
6. New private helper
   `pivotLoop_singularStep_some_of_partial_no_replacement` — by
   induction on the regular-step prefix, the row-pivoted Bareiss loop
   records the same singular index as the no-pivot loop when no
   replacement pivot is available at the triggering iteration.
7. `Matrix.bareiss_eq_bareissData_det`, `Matrix.bareissData_eq_finish_pivotLoop`
   and `Matrix.BareissData.det` — collapse the singular state to
   `Matrix.bareiss LP = 0`, hence `.toNat = 0`.

The accompanying `matrix_diag_at_fin_eq` helper is also added (Lean's
core does not give proof-irrelevant rewriting of the dependent Fin
proof term in `M[⟨v, p₁⟩][⟨v, p₁⟩] = M[⟨v, p₂⟩][⟨v, p₂⟩]`).
