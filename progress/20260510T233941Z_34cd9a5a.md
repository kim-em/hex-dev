## Notes for future readers

- Dependent `Fin n` row indexing through `Vector.getElem` triggers
  motive-abstraction failures when rewriting with equations whose proof
  arguments do not match. The workaround used here is to keep the
  invariant's row as an explicit caller-supplied `Fin n` and use `subst`
  (on a free row variable) instead of `rw` for row equalities; column
  rewrites are bridged with `congrArg (fun c : Fin m => M[r][c])`.
- The operation-level helpers from #3156 expect the OLD pivot row, and
  prove preservation for ONE column. The new-pivot helper proves the
  one-and-zeros-elsewhere statement for the freshly added column,
  combining `eliminateColumn_pivotRow` (target row unchanged) and
  `eliminateColumn_zero` (non-target rows zeroed) on top of the
  `rowScale` that normalizes the target entry to `1`.
