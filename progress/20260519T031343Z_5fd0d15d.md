## Headline — coeff zero-law wrapper introduction and HexPolyFp sweep

The largest single thread in this window is the introduction and
adoption of the typeclass-specialized `DensePoly.coeff_*_ring` /
`coeff_*_semiring` simp wrappers, replacing the prior `coeff_add _ _
_ <hzero>` shape that required threading explicit `0 + 0 = 0`
discharge hypotheses through every call site.

* **#5169** introduced `DensePoly.coeff_add_semiring`,
  `coeff_sub_ring`, `coeff_neg_ring` in `HexPoly/Operations.lean`.
* **#5188** fixed the LHS shape so the wrappers actually fire as
  rewrites — initially they used the `Lean.Grind` operation
  projections rather than the caller-facing `Add` / `Sub` / `Neg`
  instances, so `rw` / `simp` did not match the goals they were
  supposed to close. The fix added compatibility typeclasses
  `AddZeroLaw` / `SubZeroLaw` / `ZeroSubNegLaw` in
  `HexPoly/Operations.lean`, with the matching `ZMod64 p` instances in
  `HexPolyFp/Basic.lean`, plus build-time smoke theorems that lock the
  new shape in.

Once the wrappers fired, a sweep migrated all `DensePoly.coeff_(add|
sub|neg) _ _ _ <hyp>` call sites in the HexPolyFp tree, plus one
HexBerlekamp file:

* **#5198** HexPolyFp/Compose.lean (12 sites).
* **#5200** HexPolyFp/Basic.lean (57 generic-shape sites → 0).
* **#5202** HexPolyFp/SquareFree.lean.
* **#5203** HexPolyFp/Quotient.lean + QuotientFrobenius.lean.
* **#5208** HexBerlekamp/RabinSoundness.lean (34 sites).
* **#5212** residual `coeff_neg` / `coeff_add` sites in
  HexPolyFp/Compose.lean and HexGfqRing/Operations.lean.

The migration also closed obsolete private helpers
(`zmod64_zero_sub_zero`, `zmod64_zero_add`, `zmod64_zero_add_zero`,
`zmod_sub_zero_zero`, several `hzero_*` `have`-bindings) as their
last callers vanished. Two ripple patterns are recorded in the

`coeff_neg`'s `0 - x` (the `rfl` step in
