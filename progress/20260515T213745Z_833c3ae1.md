  (`recombinationSearchModAux_isSome_of_step`,
  `_eq_some_of_step_of_prefix_none`,
  `recombinationSearchModAux_one`).
- Established that the issue's literal hypothesis list is insufficient.
  The proof sketch in the issue body uses
  `liftedSubsetSplit_mem_subsetSplitsWithFirst_of_matches` with the
  `S` returned by `hrest.exists_subset hirr hdvd` — this requires
  `J.min' hne ∈ S`, which is not guaranteed for an arbitrary
  irreducible factor of `target` and is not derivable from
  `hrest`'s `exists_subset` / `unique_subset` alone.
- The recursion's transport step also can't be discharged from
  `hrest` alone: `henselSubsetCorrespondenceRest_transport_of_disjoint`
  takes a pairwise-disjointness hypothesis as input, and its
  doc-comment (lines 1694–1697) explicitly notes "Later coverage
  proofs discharge that from square-free factorisation/associatedness;
  this lemma packages the pure rest-state transport".

So two structural facts are missing at the API surface:
  (a) cover-at-min: every non-empty `J` has some irreducible divisor
      of `target` whose representing subset contains `J.min'`;
  (b) pairwise disjoint: representing subsets of non-associated
      irreducible factors of `target` (within `J`) are disjoint.

Both ultimately follow from `target` being square-free mod `p`, but
that's a Hensel-lift property not surfaced through hrest.
