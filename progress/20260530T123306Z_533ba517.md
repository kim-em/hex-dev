## Accomplished

Added `Polynomial.roots_count_radius_le_of_schmeisserComposition_source` to
`HexPolyZMathlib/SchurSzego.lean`, deriving the closed-radius count-domination
conclusion from the open-radius Grace-Walsh-Szego / de Bruijn-Springer source
hypothesis `graceWalshSzegoOpenZeroControlAtDegree n`.  The conclusion shape
matches the deliverable in #5734:

```lean
((schmeisserComposition n f g).roots.filter fun ζ => r ≤ ‖ζ‖).card ≤
  ((f.roots.filter fun z => r ≤ ‖z‖).card)
```

The wrapper closes the open-to-closed gap by selecting, for any positive `r`
and any two finite multisets `s`, `t` of complex numbers, a threshold
`ρ ∈ (0, r)` greater than every norm in `s ∪ t` that is itself `< r`.  At
that `ρ`, the open-radius filter coincides with the closed-radius filter on
both multisets, so the open-source hypothesis applied at `ρ` discharges the
closed conclusion at `r`.  The witness is built by appending `0` to the
union of below-`r` norms and taking `List.maximum_of_length_pos`.

The witness builder is encapsulated as
`exists_open_radius_witness_for_closed_count` (private).  The predicate-level
companion `Polynomial.graceWalshSzegoZeroControlAtDegree_of_open` lifts the
open Grace-Walsh-Szego source predicate to the closed one and is exposed for
downstream consumers (notably #5735's `graceWalshSzegoZeroControlAtDegree_of_pos`).
