## Accomplished

Landed SPEC Group D / D2 composition theorem
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib.choosePrimeData?_none_implies_huge` in
`HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib/HotPathDiscriminant.lean`. Given a
`ZPoly` `f`, a small prime `p ∈ [3, 500]`, and the failure of the
executable prime search `Hex.choosePrimeData? f = none`, the theorem
concludes
`(p : Int) ∣ lc(f) · Polynomial.resultant (toPolynomial f) (toPolynomial f).derivative n (n-1)`.

The proof composes three landed prerequisites:
- `Hex.mem_hotPathCandidates_isGoodPrime_false_of_choosePrimeData?_none`
  (new in this PR, in `HexBerlekampZassenhaus/Basic.lean`): when the
  executable's two-stage fold yields `none`, every hot-path candidate
  fails `isGoodPrime`.
- `Hex.exists_mem_hotPathCandidates_of_prime` (#6641): every prime in
  the SPEC range appears in the hot-path list.
- `HexBerlekampZassenhausMathlib.isGoodPrime_false_implies_dvd_resultant_deriv`
  (#6648): a per-prime failure of `isGoodPrime` forces the modulus to
  divide `lc · resultant(f, f')`.

The provenance helper required exposing the previously-`private`
structure `Hex.SmallPrimeCandidate` and list `Hex.hotPathCandidates` so
that the Mathlib-side composition theorem can pull a candidate out by
its `.p` and `.bounds` fields. The exposing change is minimal — the
fold helpers themselves stay `private`.

The fold-`none` direction is structured as three small private lemmas:
- `primeChoiceDataScore_eq_none_iff`: candidate scores yield `none` iff
  `isGoodPrime f c.p = false`.
- `choosePrimeDataScore_fold_some_ne_none`: once the fold's `best`
  state goes `some`, it stays `some`.
- `choosePrimeDataScore_fold_none_forall_isGoodPrime_false`: by
  induction on the candidate list, fold-`none` forces every entry to
  have failed `isGoodPrime`.

The public composition then walks the two-stage definition of
`choosePrimeData?` (small-prime prefix + extended list) and dispatches
to the per-list fold lemma.

The signature includes `_hp : Hex.ZPoly.Primitive f` and
`_hs : Hex.ZPoly.SquareFreeRat f` per the issue body. These are
downstream-consumer preconditions; the divisibility conclusion itself
does not depend on them.
